Science

Science is a body of knowledge about the natural world.

Branches of Science include:

  • Physics: The study of the fundamental laws of nature.
  • Chemistry: The study of matter.
  • Biology: The study of life.
  • Geology: The study of the Earth.
  • Astronomy: The study of stars, planets, and other objects in space.

Scientific Method

Science isn’t just knowledge. It also includes techniques for creating and testing knowledge.

Experiments are a key part of the Scientific Method.

  • If we understand how some aspect of nature works, we should be able to use that knowledge to make predictions.
  • Experiments test whether our predictions are correct.
  • Experiments help us find the limitations of our existing knowledge and give us the opportunity to develop better theories.

Experimental Variables

Variables are things that we measure when we perform an experiment.

  • Most scientific experiments test a hypothesis — a prediction about how changing one variable will affect another.
  • “If we increase the length of a simple pendulum, the time it takes the pendulum to complete a swing will increase.”
  • The variable that we are intentionally changing (length) is called the manipulated (or independent) variable.
  • The variable being affected (time) is called the responding (or dependent) variable.

Controlled Variables

To test our hypothesis properly, the manipulated variable is the only one that can be allowed to change.

  • If we change the mass and starting height of the pendulum as well as its length, we have no way of knowing which change caused the swing time to change.
  • We must keep the mass and starting height constant.
  • Variables that are deliberately kept constant are called controlled variables.
  • Failing to control variables properly makes the result of an experiment invalid.
