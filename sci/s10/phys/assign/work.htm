Review

    1. State the work-energy theorem and explain what it means.
      2. Work (the change in mechanical energy caused by a force) is equal to the force multiplied by the object’s displacement: $$W = \vec{\mathbf F} \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}$$
    2. Explain how to calculate the work done by a force that is not constant.
      3. Calculate the area under a graph of \(\vec{\mathbf F}\) versus \(\vec{\mathbf d}\).

Practice

  1. A baseball is moving with 24.0 joules of kinetic energy. The batter hits the ball, after which the baseball’s kinetic energy is 82.0 J.
    1. How much work did the batter do on the baseball?
      2. $$W = \Delta E_k = E_{kf} - E_{ki} = \mathrm{82.0\ J - 24.0\ J = 58.0\ J}$$
    2. What was the average force of the bat acting on the ball, if the bat and ball were in contact with each other through a displacement of 1.40 metres?
      3. $$W = \vec{\mathbf F}_{avg} \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{avg} = \frac{W}{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\frac{58.0\ J}{1.40\ m} = 41.4\ N}$$
    1. A hockey player exerts an average force of 9.40 newtons on the puck. The puck moves through a displacement of 40.0 cm while in contact with the hockey stick. How much work did the player do on the puck?
      2. $$W = \vec{\mathbf F} \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{9.40\ N \cdot 0.400\ m = 3.76\ J}$$
    2. If the puck was moving with a kinetic energy of 3.50 J before the hockey stick made contact, what was the puck’s kinetic energy after being shot?
      3. $$W = \Delta E_k = E_{kf} - E_{ki}$$ $$E_{kf} = E_{ki} + W = \mathrm{3.50\ J + 3.76\ J = 7.26\ J}$$
  3. As a block slides 85.0 cm down a ramp, friction causes the block to lose 75.0 J of mechanical energy. Calculate the force of friction.
    4. $$W = \vec{\mathbf F} \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F} = \frac{W}{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\frac{-75.0\ J}{0.850\ m} = -88.2\ N}$$

  4. The graph below shows the force exerted by one bumper car on another during a collision.

    1. Calculate the work experienced by the car.
      2. $$W = \mathrm{Area = \frac{1}{2} \times Base \times Height = \frac{1}{2} (0.40\ m) (360\ N) = 72\ J}$$
    2. If the bumper car was moving with 300 J of kinetic energy before the collision, how much kinetic energy would it have after the collision?
      3. $$W = \Delta E_k = E_{kf} - E_{ki}$$ $$E_{kf} = E_{ki} + W = \mathrm{300\ J + 72\ J = 372\ J}$$
  5. A 65.0 kg cyclist is riding at a speed of 4.20 m/s. The cyclist pedals harder, causing her speed to increase to 6.35 m/s.
    1. Calculate the cyclist’s initial kinetic energy.
      2. $$E_{ki} = \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 = \mathrm{\frac{1}{2} (65.0\ kg)(4.20\ m/s)^2 = 573\ J}$$
    2. Calculate the cyclist’s final kinetic energy.
      3. $$E_{kf} = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 = \mathrm{\frac{1}{2} (65.0\ kg)(6.35\ m/s)^2 = 1310\ J}$$
    3. How much work did the cyclist do while accelerating?
      4. $$W = \Delta E_k = E_{kf} - E_{ki} = \mathrm{1310\ J - 573\ J= 737\ J}$$
    4. Estimate the average force that the cyclist exerted if the pedals moved 4.25 metres while she was accelerating.
      5. $$W = \vec{\mathbf F} \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F} = \frac{W}{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\frac{737\ J}{4.25\ m} = 173\ N}$$
  6. A 32.5 kg child climbs into his toboggan at the top of a hill that is 5.60 m high and 25.3 m long. Gravity causes the child to begin sliding down the hill. The speed of the child and toboggan at the bottom of the hill is 7.20 m/s.
    1. Calculate the mechanical energy of the child at the top of the hill.
      2. $$E_i = mgh_i = \mathrm{(32.5\ kg) (9.81\ m/s^2) (5.60\ m) = 1785\ J}$$
    2. Calculate the mechanical energy of the child at the bottom of the hill.
      3. $$E_f = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 = \mathrm{\frac{1}{2} (32.5\ kg)(7.20\ m/s)^2= 842\ J}$$
    3. Calculate the average force of friction acting on the child.
      4. $$W = \Delta E = \mathrm{842\ J - 1785\ J = -943\ J}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F} = \frac{W}{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\frac{-943\ J}{25.3\ m} = -37.3\ N}$$
