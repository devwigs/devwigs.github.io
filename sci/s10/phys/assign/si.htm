  1. Write the name of each of these units and express each measurement in the standard SI unit.
    1. 1 mm
      2. millimeter = 10–3 m
    2. 1 MV
      3. megavolt = 106 V
    3. 1 kPa
      4. kilopascal = 103 Pa
    4. 1 μs
      5. microsecond = 10–6 s
  2. Convert each of these measurements to standard SI units. Express your answer in scientific notation.
    1. 12.1 MW
      2. 1.21 × 101 (106 W) = 1.21 × 107 W
    2. 734 pm
      3. 7.34 × 102 (10–12 m) = 7.34 × 10–10 m
    3. 0.254 ns
      4. 2.54 × 10–1 (10–9 s) = 2.54 × 10–10 s
  3. Convert each measurement to the specified unit. Express your answer in scientific notation. Show all work.
    1. 18.25 g to kg
      2. 1.825 × 101 (10–3 kg) = 1.825 × 10–2 kg
    2. 0.83 m to mm
      3. 8.3 × 10–1 (103 mm) = 8.3 × 102 mm = 830 mm
    3. 5.24 × 10–3 s to μs
      4. 5.24 × 10–3 (106 μs) = 5.24 × 103 μs
  4. The Earth orbits around the Sun with a radius of 149 600 000 km. Express this measurement in scientific notation (4 significant digits) and using the standard SI unit.
    5. 1.496 × 108 (103 m) = 1.496 × 1011 m
  5. A small amount of chemical is placed on an electronic balance and is determined to have a mass of 124 mg. Express this measurement in scientific notation and using the standard SI unit. Note: The SI unit of mass is kilograms, not grams!
    6. 1.24 × 102 mg = 1.24 × 102 (10–3 g) = 1.24 × 10–1 g = 1.24 × 10–1 (10–3 kg) = 1.24 × 10–4 kg
  6. A cyclist is travelling at 24.0 km/h. Convert this measurement to the standard SI unit for speed.
    7. 24.0 km/h = 24.0 (103 m) / (3600 s) = 6.67 m/s
  7. A sprinter can run at 11.2 m/s. How fast is this in km/h?
    8. 11.2 m/s = 11.2 (10–3 km) / (1 / 3600 h) = 40.3 km/h
  8. A coin has an area of 3.72 cm2. Express this area in the standard SI unit.
    9. 3.72 cm2 = 3.72 (10–2 m)2 = 3.72 × 10–4 m2
  9. Copper has a density of 8.9 g/cm3. Convert this measurement to the standard SI unit (kg/m3).
    10. 8.9 g/cm3 = 8.9 (10–3 kg) / (10–2 m)3 = 8.9 × 10–3 kg / (10–6 m3) = 8.9 × 103 kg/m3
