Review

  1. What is a unit? Why are units important in science?
    2. A unit is a standard of measurement that scientists have agreed to use. Stanrdized uits allow scientists to share their measurements with each other in a precise and reliable way.
  2. What does SI stand for? What does it mean?
    3. SI is the Système Internationale, or International System of Units. It is the set of units used by scientists.
  3. State the 7 fundamental SI units and what quantity each unit measures.
    4. Metres (Distance), Amperes (Electric current), Seconds (Time), Kilograms (Mass), Kelvins (Temperature), Moles (Qunatity of Matter), Candelas (Luminous Intensity)
  4. What is a derived unit?
    5. Derived units are units that are made by combining fundamental units (like m/s for speed or kg/m3 for density) or adding a prefix to make a larger or smaller unit (like microseconds). Sometimes derived units are given their own name. For example, kg·m/s2 is called a newton. Other named derived units are joules, watts, pascals, volts, coulombs, and teslas.
  5. Explain how prefixes can be used to make larger or smaller units.

    6. Prefixes are based on powers of 10. For example, kilo- means 103 or 1000 and mega- means 106 or one million. Adding these prefixes makes a larger unit; one megawatt equals one mmillion watts.

    Other prefixes make smaller units. For example, micro- means 10–6 or one-millionth. A microsecond is one millionth of a second.

  6. Explain (with examples) how to convert units by removing or inserting a prefix.

    7. Decide what the equivalence is between the unit you are trying to remove and the unit you are converting to. Then multiply the original measurement by a fraction where the numerator and denominator are the equivalent units. Decide which unit goes “on top” and which goes “underneath” so that the unit being removed cancels. If a unit is squared or cubed, you must do the same to the fraction before you can cancel.

    Example: Convert 1.50 g/cm3 to kg/m3...

    Answer: Since \(\mathrm{kg = 10^3\ g}\) and \(\mathrm{cm = 10^{-2}\ m}\)... $$\mathrm{1.50\ \frac{g}{cm^3} = 1.50\ \frac{g}{cm^3} \color{blue}{\left(\frac{kg}{10^3\ g}\right) \left(\frac{cm}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^3} = 1.50\times 10^3\ kg/m^3}$$

  4. The Earth orbits around the Sun with a radius of 149 600 000 km. Express this measurement in scientific notation (4 significant digits) and using the standard SI unit.
    5. $$\mathrm{1.496\times 10^8\ km = 1.496\times 10^8\ km \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^3\ m}{km}\right)} = 1.496\times 10^{11}\ m}$$
  5. A small amount of chemical is placed on an electronic balance and is determined to have a mass of 124 mg. Express this measurement in scientific notation and using the standard SI unit. Note: The SI unit of mass is kilograms, not grams!
    6. $$\mathrm{1.24\times 10^2\ mg = 1.24\times 10^2\ mg \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^{-3}\ g}{mg}\right)\left(\frac{kg}{10^3\ g}\right)} = 1.24\times 10^{-4}\ kg}$$
  6. A cyclist is travelling at 24.0 km/h. Convert this measurement to the standard SI unit for speed.
    7. $$\mathrm{24.0\ km/h = 24.0\ km/h \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^3\ m}{km}\right)\left(\frac{h}{3600\ s}\right)} = 6.67\ m/s}$$
  7. A sprinter can run at 11.2 m/s. How fast is this in km/h?
    8. $$\mathrm{11.2\ m/s = 11.2\ m/s \color{blue}{\left(\frac{km}{10^3\ m}\right)\left(\frac{3600\ s}{h}\right)} = 40.3\ km/h}$$
  8. A coin has an area of 3.72 cm2. Express this area in the standard SI unit.
    9. $$\mathrm{3.72\ cm^2 = 3.72\ cm^2 \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^{-2}\ m}{cm}\right)^2} = 3.72\times 10^{-4}\ m^2}$$
  9. Copper has a density of 8.9 g/cm3. Convert this measurement to the standard SI unit (kg/m3).
    10. $$\mathrm{8.9\ \frac{g}{cm^3} = 8.9\ \frac{g}{cm^3} \color{blue}{\left(\frac{kg}{10^3\ g}\right) \left(\frac{cm}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^3} = 8.9\times 10^3\ kg/m^3}$$
