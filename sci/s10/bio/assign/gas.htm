Review

    1. What are the stomata? Where are they found? What is their function?
      2. Openings in the bottom of the leaf that allow gases (oxygen, carbon dioxide, water) in and out.
    2. Draw a labelled illustration of a stoma with its guard cells.
  2. Define transpiration.
    3. Evaporation of water from the leaf.
  3. Explain how the guard cells respond to wet or dry conditions to regulate gas exchange.
    4. Turgor pressure (wet conditions) causes these cells to expand which opens the stomata. Plasmolysis (dry conditions) cause the cells to shrink which closes the stomata.
  4. Describe three important gases that need to be exchanged for the plant to remain healthy and explain how each gas is related to photosynthesis or cellular respiration.

    5. Oxygen: Produced by photosynthesis and consumed by cellular respiration.

    Carbon dioxide: Consumed by photosynthesis and produced by cellular respiration.

    Water: Consumed by photosynthesis and produced by cellular respiration.

  5. What is a lenticel and what is its role in gas exchange?
    6. A small hole in the trunk of a tree or woody plant that promotes gas exchange.
