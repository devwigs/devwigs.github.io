Cell Specialization

  1. Explain the difference between unicellular and multicellular organisms.
    2. Unicellular organisms consist of a single cell that must perform all of the organism’s functions. Multicellular organisms consist of many cells that are specialized to perform only certain of life’s functions.
  2. Define:
    1. tissue
    2. organ
    3. system

    3. tissue: Cells of a similar type and function (e.g. muscle, palisade cells)

    organ: Different tissues that work together to perform a specific function(s) for the organism (e.g. liver, leaf)

    system: One or more organs and tissues that function together to meet the organism’s needs (e.g. vascular or circulatory system)

  3. Describe four different tissues that are present in plant leaves.

    4. Epidermis: a layer of cells covering the outside of the leaf

    Palisade: a tightly packed layer(s) of cells where photosynthesis occurs

    Spongy Mesophyll: a loosely packed region that allows for gas exchange

    Guard Cells: cells that open and close the stomata to regulate gas exchange

    Xylem/Phloem: cells responsible for water / sap transport

  4. Identify three plant organs.
    5. Leaf, Root, Stem, Flower
  5. Identify three animal organs.
    6. Heart, Liver, Brain, Lung, Skin, Kidney

Photosynthesis

  1. In what kinds of organisms does photosynthesis occur?
    2. Plants and some single-celled organisms
  2. What is the chemical equation for photosynthesis?
    3. $$\mathrm{6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2}$$ [carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen]
  3. Where in the cell (i.e. in which organelle) does photosynthesis occur?
    4. Chloroplast
  4. Why is photosynthesis so important?
    5. This allows the plant to store energy from sunlight which it needs to survive.
  5. What is cellular respiration? In which organelle does it occur?
    6. This is the opposite of photosynthesis. The glucose is reacted to release the energy in the form of a chemical ATP. It occurs it the mitochondria.
  6. What is the chemical equation for cellular respiration?
    7. $$\mathrm{C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6\ O_2 \rightarrow 6\ CO_2 + 6\ H_2O}$$
  7. How is cellular respiration similar to/different than combustion?
    8. The reaction is very similar but it occurs much more slowly.
  8. What is ATP? What is its connection to photosynthesis and cellular respiration?
    9. It is a chemical that the cell uses to store energy for immediate use.
