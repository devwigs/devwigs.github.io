Review

  1. Define and give examples of active transport.
    2. Motion of substances across a membrane that requires energy. This happens when material needs to moved from lower to higher concentration. Passive transport (no energy needed) only works from high to low. Examples of active transport include endocytosis and exocytosis.
  2. Define endocytosis and draw an illustration.
    3. This is when the membrane engulfs the material to be brought into the cell. The membrane “pit” pinches off to form a vesicle.
  3. Define exocytosis and draw an illustration.
    4. This is the reverse of endocytosis, where a vesicle containing material to be moved outside the cell joins to the cell membrane and expels the material.
    1. Define phagocytosis.
      2. Endocytosis where the material entering the cell is solid (food)
    2. Define pinocytosis.
      3. Endocytosis where the material entering the cell is liquid (water)
