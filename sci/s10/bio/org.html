Organelles

The parts of the cell are called organelles.

Each organelle performs one or more essential functions for the cell.

  • Cytoplasm is the fluid in which the other organelles exist.
  • The cell membrane is a thin layer that separates/contains the cell from its surroundings.
  • Vacuoles are storage areas that contain water, nutrients, or waste.
  • Lysosomes are small vacuoles (vesicles) containing digestive fluids.
  • The nucleus contains the cells genetic material (DNA).
  • The nucleolus is a part of the nucleus that makes ribosomes.
  • Ribosomes are structures that build and transport proteins and other molecules.
  • Endoplasmic reticulum is a network of membranes connected to the nucleus. The rough ER is studded with ribosomes while the smooth ER makes lipids and other molecules.
  • The Golgi apparatus sorts and repackages molecules and distributes them in vesicles.
  • Mitochondria are structures responsible for cellular respiration, where food is transformed into other forms of energy.

Plant Organelles

Plant cells have some organelles not found in animals...

  • Chloroplasts are organelles that contain chlorophyll and perform photosynthesis.
  • The cell wall is a strong, cellulose support around the outside of the cell.
  • Many plant cells have a very large central vacuole.

Animal Organelles

One organelle found in animal cells usually does not occur in plants...

  • Centrioles are used in cell division (reproduction) to ensure that each daughter cell gets an accurate copy of the cells genetic material.
