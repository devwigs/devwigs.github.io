Science 10

Diffusion

  • Brownian motion is the random motion of molecules that occurs in liquids and gases.
  • The motion of molecules is faster at higher temperatures.
  • Over time, Brownian motion causes the concentration of substances to become uniform.
  • There is a net movement of material down the concentration gradient (from higher to lower concentrations).
  • This effect is called diffusion.

Passive Transport

Passive transport means movement across a membrane that does not require any energy expenditure by the cell.

  1. Simple diffusion is a type of passive transport that allows movement of small molecules (e.g. O2 and CO2) that can fit between the phospholipid molecules that make up the membrane.
  2. Osmosis refers to the diffusion of water.
  3. Facilitated diffusion refers to diffusion of larger molecules (e.g. glucose) or ions that would be repelled by the phospholipids. This requires the help of transport proteins.
  • Some molecules cannot pass through the membrane at all; biological membranes are semi-permeable.

Osmosis

Osmosis is diffusion of water molecules through the membrane.

  • When the cell’s environment (surroundings) have a higher concentration of solutes (lower concentration of water) than in the cell...
  • ...the surroundings are called hypertonic.
  • Water will move out of the cell causing the cell to shrink.
  • Cell shrinkage due to osmosis is called plasmolysis.

  • When the cell’s environment have a lower concentration of solutes (higher concentration of water) than in the cell...
  • ...the surroundings are called hypotonic.
  • Water will move into of the cell causing the cell to expand.
  • The outward pressure due to osmosis is called turgour pressure.

  • When the concentrations are balanced inside and outside the cell, the surroundings are called isotonic.
  • There is no net movement of water.

Facilitated Diffusion

Facilitated diffusion is diffusions through transport proteins embedded in the membrane.

  • Channel proteins allow diffusion of ions that cannot pass through the phospholipid molecules.
  • Carrier proteins change shape to open to one side of the membrane at a time.
  • Carrier proteins allow larger molecules like glucose to diffuse through the membrane.
  • Carrier proteins can also allow molecules to be transported against the concentration gradient.
  • This is called active transport and, unlike passive transport, it requires energy.
