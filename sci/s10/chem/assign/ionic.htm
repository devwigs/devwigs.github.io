Review

  1. Define a binary ionic compound and explain how the atoms are held together in these compounds.
    2. A compound formed from atoms of two different elements. One element forms a positive ion and the other a negative ion. They are held together because their opposite charges attract. This is called an ionic bond.
  2. Define cation and anion.
    3. The cation is positive ion and the anion is the negative ion.
    1. State the Law of Conservation of Electric Charge and explain how it affects ionic compounds involving ions with unequal charges.
      2. The total charge must remain constant. This means that for an ionic compound, the charge of the cation must exactly balance the charge of the anion.
    2. Explain how to determine the chemical formula for an ionic compound.
      3. Compare the charges of the ions. The total positive charge and the total negative charge must be the LCM of the cation and anion charges.
  4. Explain how to name binary ionic compounds. Specifically include how to name compounds involving elements that can form more than one ion.
    5. Give the name of the cation first. If the element has more than one possible ion, include the ion charge as a roman numeral such as “iron(III)” or “iron(II)”. The anion name, with the suffix “-ide”, goes second.
