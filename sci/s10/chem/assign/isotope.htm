    1. What are the three particles that make up the atom?
      2. Protons, Neutrons, Electrons
    2. Where are the particles located in the atom?
      3. Protons and neutrons are found in the dense, central nucleus. Electrons orbit around the nucleus like planets around the Sun.
    3. What are the relative masses and charges of the particles?
      4. Protons and neutrons have nearly identical masses. Electrons are more than 1800 times smaller. Protons are positive and electrons are negative. Neutrons have no charge.
    1. What do we mean by atomic number?
      2. Atomic number is the number of protons. This determines what element the atom is.
    2. What do we mean by atomic mass?
      3. Total number of protons and neutrons. (The electrons have an almost negligible mass.)
  3. What property of an atom determines its chemical properties?
    4. Atomic number
  4. What do we mean by the isotopes of an element? As an example, describe the similarities and differences among atoms of carbon-12, carbon-13, and carbon-14.
    5. Atoms with the same number of protons (atomic number) but different numbers of neutron are called isotopes. They belong to the same element by have different mass numbers.
    1. What is an ion?
      2. An atom where the number of electrons is different from the number of protons is called an ion.
    2. How does the net charge of an atom or ion relate to the number of each type of particles?
      3. The net charge is the number of (positive) protons minus the number of (negative) electrons.
    3. Define cation and anion and explain how to name ions.
      4. A cation is a positive ion and an anion is negative. Cations use the same name as the atom from which they are formed, unless the atom can form more than one ion in which case the charge is included in the name as a Roman numeral; for example, copper(II) ions. For anions, the suffix is changed to -ide; e.g. chloride instead of chlorine.
  6. Explain how to write and use isotope notation? Use the sodium-23 ion as an example.
    7. $$_{11}^{23}\mathrm{Na}^+$$ The atomic number is written to the lower left of the chemical symbol. The mass number is upper left. The charge (if any) is upper right.
