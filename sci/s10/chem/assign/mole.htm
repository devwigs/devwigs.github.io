Review

  1. How many atoms of hydrogen-1 does it take to have a total mass of 1.00 gram? What do we call this number?
    2. 1.00 g of hydrogen-1 would contain 6.02 × 1023 atoms. This number is called Avogadro’s number. This quantity of atoms is called one mole.
    1. Why is it that the atomic molar mass of hydrogen is not exactly 1.00 g/ mol?
      2. Some naturally occurring atoms of hydrogen are of the isotope hydrogen-2. The average molar mass for hydrogen atoms is slightly larger than 1.00 g/mol.
    2. Why is the molecular molar mass of hydrogen twice as large as its atomic molar mass?
      3. Hydrogen occurs at SATP as a diatomic molecule, H2. The hydrogen molecule has a mass equal to two hydrogen atoms.
