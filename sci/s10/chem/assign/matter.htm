Review

  1. Explain the difference between:
    1. element and compound
      2. Elements contain only one type of atom (e.g. iron) while compounds consist of molecules or crystals made from two or more different types of atoms (e.g. NaCl or H2O)
    2. pure substance and mixture
      3. A pure substance contains only one kind of material (either an element or a compound) while a mixture contains two or more pure substances (e.g. NaCl dissolved in water).
    3. homogeneous and heterogeneous mixture
      4. A homogeneous mixture (solution) is mixed uniformly (the properties are the same everywhere) while a heterogeneous mixture is non-uniform (there are regions with different properties).
  2. What are the different components of a solution called?

    3. Solvent: Substance that dissolves the solute

    Solute: Substance that is dissolved in the solvent

  3. The components of a mixture can be separated using different methods. Describe the following methods.
    1. Distillation
      2. Heat the mixture until one of the substances boils and evaporates
    2. Paper Chromatography
      3. Different substances will move up the paper at different rates.
    3. Filtration
      4. Separate solids from liquids or two solids of different particle sizes using a filter
