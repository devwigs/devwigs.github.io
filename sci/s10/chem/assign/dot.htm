Review

  1. Define the following terms:
    1. Periodicity
      2. Periodicity refers to the idea that something (such as the properties of the elements) repeats on a regular basis.
    2. Group (Family)
      3. The elements within the same column of the periodic table are called groups (or families) and tend to have similar properties.
    3. Period
      4. The elements within the same row of the periodic table are called periods.
    4. Phase (State)
      5. The phase or state of a substance means whether it is in the form of a solid, liquid, or gas.
  2. State the essential properties of the following categories of element and where they would be found in the Periodic Table.
    1. Metals
      2. Metals tend to be ductile and malleable solids (at SATP) that conduct electricity; they are found to the left of the “staircase” in the periodic table.
    2. Non-Metals
      3. Non-metals tend to be non-ductile and non-malleable; less likely than metals to be solid or conductors; they are found to the right of the staircase in the periodic table.
    3. Metalloids
      4. Metalloids have some properties of both metals and non-metals; they are found adjacent to the staircase in the periodic table.
    4. Noble (Inert) Gases
      5. Noble gases (also called inert gases) are elements found in Group 18; they are very non-reactive gases.
    5. Halogens
      6. Halogens are elements from Group 17; they are highly reactive non-metals.
    6. Alkali Metals
      7. Alkali metals are from Group 1; they are highly reactive metals.

  4. Explain what a “Lewis dot diagram” (also called an electron dot diagram) is, how to draw one, and why it is important.
    An illustration of an atom or ion’s valence electrons. These electrons, and how they are paired, determines the chemical behaviour of the atom.
