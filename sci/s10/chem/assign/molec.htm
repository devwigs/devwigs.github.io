Review

  1. Define:
    1. binary compound
      2. A pure substance composed of atoms of two different elements.
    2. covalent bond
      3. Atoms share electrons and form molecules, held together by the shared attraction.
    3. ionic bond
      4. Electrons are transferred between atoms; the ions form a lattice due to the attraction/repulsion between them.
  2. Describe the 7+1 Rule. Also, identify the two elements that form polyatomic molecules.
    3. The seven elements in the shape of a seven, starting at element 7, all form diatomic molecules, as does hydrogen. The two elements below the top of the 7 are polyatomic (P4, S8).
  3. Explain why atoms form covalent or ionic bonds.
    4. Atoms like to gain, lose, or share valence electrons so that they have completely full valence levels.
  4. Explain how binary molecular compounds are named under the IUPAC system.
    5. The name of the element furthest left in the periodic table comes first, followed by the second element with the suffix changed to -ide. Prefixes (mono-, di-, tri-, tetra-, penta-, etc.) are used to indicate how many of each atom are contained in each molecule. The prefix mono- is omitted only on the first part of the name.
  5. Compare the properties of ionic compounds to those of molecular compounds.
    6. Ionic compounds, if they dissolve in water, are more likely to conduct electricity. Ionic compounds also tend to exist as solid crystals with high melting points.

Practice

    1. A compound dissolves in water. The resulting solution is found to be a good conductor of electricity. Is the compound more likely to be ionic or molecular?
      2. Ionic
    2. A compound dissolves in water. The resulting solution is found to be a non-conductor. Is the compound more likely to be ionic or molecular?
      3. Molecular
    3. A compound does not dissolve in water. Is the compound more likely to be ionic or molecular?
      4. There is no way to tell... there are insoluble compounds for both ionic and molecular types.
