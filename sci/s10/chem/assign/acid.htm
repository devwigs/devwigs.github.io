    1. What does pH stand for?
    2. Explain how the pH scale works.

    5. pH stands for power of hydrogen.

    In pure water, one “mole” of water molecules out of every ten million (107) litres will break apart to create a H+ and a OH– ion. Both ions have a concentration of 10–7 “moles per litre”. We define the pH of pure (neutral) water as 7. When we add acid to the water, the concentration of H+ goes up. A pH of 6, for example, means 10 times more H+ ions compared to pure water. Acids always have a pH lower than 7. Bases increase the concentration of OH– and decrease the concentration of H+; they have pH greater than 7.
