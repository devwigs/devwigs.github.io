Energy Conservation

The Law of Energy Conservation (also called the First Law of Thermodynamics) is one of the most important laws in all of Science.

Energy cannot be created or destroyed. Energy can be transformed from one form to another or transferred from one object to another.

Energy Transformations

Forces are responsible for transformations of mechanical energy.

  • The force of gravity transforms gravitational potential energy into kinetic energy as an object falls.
  • The force applied by a student’s foot transforms chemical energy (food calories) into kinetic energy when kicking a ball.
  • The force of friction transforms kinetic energy into thermal and sound energy as an object slides across a surface.

Work

The amount of mechanical energy that a force transforms is called the work done by the force.

  • If gravity causes a falling object to lose 50 joules of gravitational potential energy as it falls...
  • ...it will gain 50 joules of kinetic energy (since the total energy is conserved).
  • We say the force of gravity did 50 joules of work.
  • The work done is positive when the kinetic energy increases and negative when it decreases.

What if gravity and air resistance are both acting on the object at the same time?

  • Gravity still does 50 J of work; the object loses 50 J of gravitational potential energy.
  • Air resistance slows the object; it does a negative amount of work, say −2 J.
  • The object gains 48 J of energy (the total work done by both forces.)
  • The other 2 joules becomes sound or thermal energy.
