Biomes

A biome is a geographic region that has characteric types of flora (plants) and fauna (animals).

  • For example, cactus plants are characteristic of a desert biome while polar bears are characteristic of the tundra.
  • Biomes are also called ecozones.
  • Land-based biomes are called terrestrial biomes.
  • Water-based biomes are called aquatic biomes.
  • The term marine can be used to describe ocean (saltwater) biomes.

Biomes & Climate

Climate (hot or cold, wet or dry) is a key factor in determining which organisms are present.

  • Cactus plants are found in hot, dry areas.
  • Polar bears are found in cold, dry areas adjacent to the ocean.
  • Plants and animals are less likely to thrive in environments to which they are not adapted.

Adaptations

Features that plants and animals have that make them suited to their environment are called adaptations.

  • Physiological adaptations are related to the internal workings (e.g. metabolism and other biochemistry) of the organism.
  • Structural adaptations are related to the physical features of an organism.
  • Behavioural adaptations refer to how an animals behaviour helps it survive and thrive in its environment.

Polar bears can communicate through body language and vocalizations... Behavioual

Polar bears have white fur that camouflage them against the snow in the tundra, helping them to hunt for food... Structural

Some polar bears hibernate during the long, cold winter when food is scarce... Physiological
