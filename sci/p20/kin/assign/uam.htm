Eqn. 13$$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \frac{{\vec{\mathbf v}}_i + {\vec{\mathbf v}}_f}{2} \Delta t$$

  1. During takeoff, a jet aircraft can accelerate at 3.25 m/s2. The jet must reach a velocity of 360 km/h before it reaches the end of the runway. Calculate:
    1. the minimum time that it would take the jet to reach takeoff speed from rest.
    2. the minimum length of runway needed.
    \(\vec{\mathbf a} / \mathrm{(m/s^2)}\)+3.25
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_i / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)0.00
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)+100
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} / \mathrm{m}\)
    \(\Delta t / \mathrm{s}\)

    Solve for \(\Delta t\) in Eqn 12: $$\Delta t = \frac{{\vec{\mathbf v}}_f - {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{+100\ m/s - 0.00\ m/s}{+3.25\ m/s^2} = 30.8\ s}$$

    Evaluate \(\Delta \vec{\mathbf d}\) with Eqn 13: $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{\frac{0.00\ m/s + 100.0\ m/s}{2} 30.8\ s = +1.54\times 10^3\ m}$$

  2. A car travels 185 metres while accelerating from 60.0 km/h to 80.0 km/h. Calculate the car’s acceleration and the amount of time it takes to accelerate.
    \(\vec{\mathbf a} / \mathrm{(m/s^2)}\)
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_i / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)+16.7
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)+22.2
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} / \mathrm{m}\)+185
    \(\Delta t / \mathrm{s}\)

    Solve for \(\Delta t\) in Eqn 13 after converting km/h to m/s: $$\Delta t = \frac{2\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}}{{\vec{\mathbf v}}_i + {\vec{\mathbf v}}_f} = \mathrm{\frac{2\cdot(+185\ m)}{+16.7\ m/s + 22.2\ m/s} = 9.51\ s}$$

    Evaluate \(\vec{\mathbf a}\) with Eqn 12: $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{\frac{+22.2\ m/s - 16.7\ m/s}{9.51\ s} = +0.584\ m/s^2}$$

  3. A skydiver falls 50.0 metres in 2.05 seconds, while accelerating at 6.00 m/s2. [Remember that the directions of acceleration and displacement are negative.]
    \(\vec{\mathbf a} / \mathrm{(m/s^2)}\)–6.00
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_i / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} / \mathrm{m}\)–50.0
    \(\Delta t / \mathrm{s}\)2.05

    Solve for \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_i\) in Eqn 15: $$\begin{align}{\vec{\mathbf v}}_i &= \frac{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} - \frac{1}{2} \vec{\mathbf a} (\Delta t)^2}{\Delta t} \\&= \mathrm{\frac{-50.0\ m - \frac{1}{2}(-6.00\ m/s^2)(2.05\ s)^2}{2.05\ s}} \\&= \mathrm{-18.2\ m/s}\end{align}$$

    Solve for \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f\) in Eqn 12: $$\begin{align}{\vec{\mathbf v}}_f &= \vec{\mathbf a}\Delta t + {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i\\&= \mathrm{-6.00\ m/s^2\cdot 2.05\ s - 18.2\ m/s}\\&= \mathrm{-30.5\ m/s}\end{align}$$

  4. A ball is dropped (i.e. \({\vec{\mathbf{v}}}_i = 0\)) from the top of a 35.0-metre tall building. The ball accelerates downward at 9.53 m/s2 due to the combined forces of gravity and air resistance.
    \(\vec{\mathbf a} / \mathrm{(m/s^2)}\)–9.53
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_i / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)0.00
    \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f / \mathrm{(m/s)}\)
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} / \mathrm{m}\)–35.0
    \(\Delta t / \mathrm{s}\)

    Solve for \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f\) in Eqn 14: $$\begin{align} v_f &= \sqrt{v_i^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\&= \mathrm{\sqrt{0 + 2(-9.53\ m/s^2)\cdot(-35.0\ m)}} \\&= \mathrm{\pm 25.8\ m/s} \end{align}$$ Use the negative answer because the ball is falling!

    Solve for \(\Delta t\) in Eqn 12: $$\Delta t = \frac{{\vec{\mathbf v}}_f - {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{-25.8\ m/s - 0.00\ m/s}{-9.53\ m/s^2} = 2.71\ s}$$

    Solve for \({\vec{\mathbf v}}_f\) in Eqn 12: $$\begin{align}{\vec{\mathbf v}}_f &= \vec{\mathbf a}\Delta t + {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i \\&= \mathrm{+20.0\ m/s^2\cdot 32.0\ s + 2.68\times 10^3\ m/s}\\&= \mathrm{+3.32\times 10^3\ m/s}\end{align}$$

    1. The driver of a car sees that a bridge 285 m ahead is washed out. The driver immediately hits the brakes, causing the car to accelerate at –2.50 m/s2. If the car just barely stops in time, calculate its initial velocity and braking time.

    2. Suppose the driver has a “reaction time” of 0.650 seconds.       3.       Evaluate \(\vec{\mathbf a}\) with Eqn 12: $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{\frac{0.00\ m/s - 37.7\ m/s}{13.8\ s} = -2.73\ m/s^2}$$ The driver has to accelerate more severely and stop more quickly to compensate for their reaction time! Note that the 13.8 seconds stopping time is in addition to the 0.650 s reaction time.
