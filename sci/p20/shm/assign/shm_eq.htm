  1. A mass executes SHM with a period of 0.36 s and an amplitude of 4.0 cm.
    1. Calculate the frequency of the SHM.
      2. $$f = \frac{1}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{0.36\ s} = 2.8\ Hz}$$
    2. Calculate the maximum velocity and maximum acceleration of the mass.
      3. $$v_{max} = \frac{2\pi A}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 0.040\ m}{0.36\ s} = 0.70\ m/s}$$ $$a_{max} = \frac{4\pi^2 A}{T^2} = \mathrm{\frac{4\pi^2\cdot 0.040\ m}{(0.36\ s)^2} = 12\ m/s^2}$$
    3. Draw accurate graphs of \(\vec{\mathbf{x}}\), \(\vec{\mathbf{v}}\) and \(\vec{\mathbf{a}}\) for two complete periods.

  2. When a car hits a bump in the road, it executes SHM (it bounces up and down on the springs in its shock absorbers.) If the maximum velocity of the SHM is 25.0 cm/s and the maximum acceleration is 1.50 m/s2, determine the period, frequency, and amplitude of the SHM.
    3. Calculate the amplitude: $$a_{max} = \frac{v_{max}^2}{A}$$ $$a_{max} A = v_{max}^2$$ $$A = \frac{v_{max}^2}{a_{max}} = \mathrm{\frac{(0.250\ m/s))^2}{1.50\ m/s^2} = 0.0417\ m}$$ Calculate the period: $$v_{max} = \frac{2\pi A}{T}$$ $$v_{max}T = 2\pi A$$ $$T = \frac{2\pi A}{v_{max}}= \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 0.0417\ m}{0.250\ m/s} = 1.05\ s}$$ Calculate frequency: $$f = \frac{1}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{1.05\ s} = 0.955\ Hz}$$
  3. A pendulum has an amplitude of 42.0 cm and a maximum acceleration of 1.34 m/s2. Calculate the pendulumâ€™s period, frequency, and maximum velocity.

Calculate period: $$a_{max} = \frac{4\pi^2 A}{T^2}$$ $$a_{max} T^2 = 4\pi^2 A$$ $$T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 A}{a_{max}}$$ $$T = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2 A}{a_{max}}} = \mathrm{\sqrt{\frac{4\pi^2\cdot 0.420\ m}{1.34\ m/s^2}} = 3.52\ s}$$ Calculate frequency: $$f = \frac{1}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{3.52\ s} = 0.284\ Hz}$$ Calculate \(v_{max}\): $$v_{max} = \frac{2\pi A}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 0.420\ m}{3.52\ s} = 0.750\ m/s}$$
  4. When a tuning fork is struck with a hammer, it begins to vibrate in SHM with an amplitude of 1.8 mm and a frequency of 360 Hz.
    1. Calculate the period of the motion.
      2. $$T = \frac{1}{f} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{360\ Hz} = 0.00278\ s}$$
    2. Draw and label an accurate graph of the motion for two complete periods.
      3. Graph
    1. A 50.0 g mass hangs vertically from a spring. Calculate the elastic force that the spring exerts on the mass when the mass is at equilibrium*. Include a free body diagram. (*Recall that this means the forces are balanced.)
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_g = m\vec{\mathbf g} = \mathrm{0.0500\ kg (-9.81\ m/s^2) = -0.491\ N}$$ Since the net force is zero, the elastic force is \(\vec{\mathbf F}_{elas}\) = +0.491 N.
    2. When the mass is pulled 1.00 cm down from its equilibrium position, the spring exerts an upward elastic force of 0.720 N. Calculate the restoring force that acts on the mass.
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_{elas} = \mathrm{(-0.491\ N) + 0.720\ N = +0.230\ N}$$
    3. Calculate the acceleration of the mass. Will the acceleration remain constant? Describe the motion of the mass.
      4. $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+0.230\ N}{0.0500\ kg} = +4.59\ m/s^2}$$ This acceleration will not be constant since the elastic force changes as the spring becomes more or less stretched. The mass will oscillate in SHM.
    4. Calculate the speed of the mass as it passes through equilibrium and use this speed to calculate the kinetic energy.
      5. $$a_{max} = \frac{v_{max}^2}{A}$$ $$a_{max} A = v_{max}^2$$ $$v_{max} = \sqrt{a_{max} A} = \mathrm{\sqrt{4.59\ m/s^2\cdot 0.0100\ m} = 0.214\ m/s}$$ $$E_k = \frac{1}{2} mv^2 = \mathrm{\frac{1}{2} (0.0500\ kg) (0.214\ m/s)^2 = 0.00115\ J}$$
    5. Verify that the kinetic energy calculated in (d) is equal to the work done by the restoring force in moving the mass from its initial position (\(\vec{\mathbf{x}} = \mathrm{-1.00\ cm}\)) to its final position (\(\vec{\mathbf{x}} = \mathrm{0.00\ cm}\)).
      6. First, calculate the average net force: $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{avg} = \mathrm{\frac{0.230\ N + 0.00\ N}{2} = 0.115\ N}$$ Now calculate the work: $$W = \vec{\mathbf F}_{avg}\cdot \Delta \vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{0.115\ N\cdot 0.0100\ m = 0.00115\ J}$$
