Physics 20

Power

The rate at which a force does work is called power.

  • Power is a measure of how quickly an energy-transforming device is able to do work:$$P = {\Delta E\over \Delta t}$$
  • The unit of power is J/s, which is called watts (W).
  • Do not confuse the symbol for the unit W (watts) with the symbol for the variable W (work).

Efficiency

Most energy transformations produce unwanted “waste” forms of energy.

  • For example, a light bulb is intended to transform electrical energy into light energy. In reality, a significant fraction of the energy is wasted in the form of heat.

Scientists use the concept of efficiency to describe the fraction or percentage of the energy input that is transformed into its intended or useful output:$$\epsilon = {\Delta E_{useful} \over |\Delta E_{input}|}$$

  • To say that a light bulb is 20% efficient means that 20% of the electrical energy consumed becomes light energy, and the remaining 80% of the energy consumed becomes waste (thermal energy).

Example

A 2000 kg car accelerates from rest to 20.0 m/s. The engine has an efficiency of 32.0% and produces 120 horsepower (1 hp = 746 W).

  1. How long does it take the car to reach its final speed if air resistance and friction are ignored?

Calculate the work done by the engine as the change in kinetic energy...

$$E_{ki} = {1\over 2}mv_i^2 = 0\ {\mathrm J}$$

$$E_{kf} = {1\over 2}mv_f^2 = \mathrm{{1\over 2}(2000\ kg) (20.0\ m/s)^2 = 400000\ J}$$

$$W = \Delta E_k = \mathrm{400000\ J}$$

Use the power equation to solve for time...

$$P = {\Delta E \over \Delta t}$$

$$P\Delta t = \Delta E$$

$$\Delta t = {\Delta E\over P} = \mathrm{{400000\ J\over 89520\ W} = 4.47\ s}$$

  • Realistically, the engine does not operate at its maximum power all the time, so it would almost certainly take longer than calculated!
  1. How much chemical energy (gasoline) is used in accelerating the car?

$$\epsilon = {\Delta E_{useful} \over \Delta E_{input}}$$

$$\epsilon \Delta E_{input} = \Delta E_{useful}$$

$$\Delta E_{input} = {\Delta E_{useful}\over \epsilon} = \mathrm{{400000\ J\over 0.320} = 1.25\ MJ}$$

  • The efficiency is a little under one-third, so a little less than one-third of the 1.25 MJ consumed becomes useful energy (0.40 MJ).
  • The remaining 0.85 MJ represents the sound and thermal energy produced by the engine.
