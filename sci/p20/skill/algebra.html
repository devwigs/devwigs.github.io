Physics 20

Equations

In Physics, relationships between variables are often described by an equation.

  • You saw many relationships in Science 10:

$$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

$$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_f - \vec{\mathbf v}_i}{\Delta t}$$

$$Q = mc\Delta T$$

  • When solving problems in Physics, you will often need to use algebra to rearrange an equation to isolate the variable that you are trying to solve for.

Algebra

The basic principle of algebra is that you can perform any mathematical operation to an equation, as long as you do the same to both sides of the equation.

$$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

  • If we are trying to isolate \(v\), we need to cancel all of the operations involving \(v\): squaring, multiplication by \(\frac{1}{2}\), and multiplication by \(m\).
  • You can “cancel” an operation by performing its inverse: addition cancels subtraction, division cancels multiplication, square rooting cancels squaring.
  • When you have more than one operation to cancel, use reverse order of operations.
  • To cancel multiplication by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal: $$\color{red}{2}E_k = \color{red}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
  • Rewrite the equation without the operations that cancel: $$2E_k = mv^2$$
  • Now perform the inverse of the next operation you want to cancel, then simplify: $$\frac{2E_k}{\color{red}{m}} = \frac{mv^2}{\color{red}{m}}$$ $$\frac{2E_k}{m} = v^2$$
  • Continue performing inverse operations and then simplifying until the variable you are solving for has been isolated: $$\color{red}{\sqrt{\color{black}{\frac{2E_k}{m}}} = \sqrt{\color{black}{v^2}}}$$ $$\sqrt{\frac{2E_k}{m}} = v$$
  • Once you’ve isolated your unknown, you can substitute values and evaluate: $$v = \mathrm{\sqrt{\frac{2\cdot 8000\ J}{65.0\ kg}} = 15.7\ m/s}$$
  • You should always check your answer by substituting it into the original equation: $$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \mathrm{\frac{1}{2}(65.0\ kg)(15.7\ m/s)^2 = 8011\ J}$$
  • This is slightly different than what we were expecting (8000 J) because we rounded our answer to three digits.
  • If we keep more decimal places (15.689291 m/s) we get 8000 joules almost exactly!
