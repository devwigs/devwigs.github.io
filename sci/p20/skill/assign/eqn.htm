  1. What do we mean by a model equation for the data collected during an experiment?

Practice

  1. Science 10 students performed an experiment for their “Heat” chapter in which they measured the temperature (\(T\)) of some water as they burned a marshmallow beneath it. They constructed a scatter plot for the temperature as a function of the time (\(t\)) for which the water was heated. They noted that their best-fit line passed through the points (0.0 s, 18 °C) and (20.0 s, 25 °C).
    1. Determine the model equation for the students’ data.
      2. $$k = \frac{T_2 - T_1}{t_2 - t_1} = \mathrm{\frac{(25 - 18)\ ^\circ C}{(20.0 - 0.0)\ s} = 0.35\ ^\circ C/s}$$ $$b = \mathrm{18\ ^\circ C}$$ $$T = (\mathrm{0.35\ ^\circ C/s})\ t + \mathrm{18\ ^\circ C}$$
    2. Use the model equation to predict the water temperature after 15.0 seconds.
      3. $$T = \mathrm{(0.35\ ^\circ C/s)\ 15.0\ s + 18\ ^\circ C = 23\ ^\circ C}$$
    3. Use the model equation to predict the water temperature after 60.0 seconds.
      4. $$T = \mathrm{(0.35\ ^\circ C/s)\ 60.0\ s + 18\ ^\circ C = 39\ ^\circ C}$$
    4. Use the model equation to predict how long it would take the water to boil (100 °C).
      5. $$T - \mathrm{18\ ^\circ C} = (\mathrm{0.35\ ^\circ C/s})\ t$$ $$t = \frac{T - \mathrm{18\ ^\circ C}}{\mathrm{0.35\ ^\circ C/s}} = \frac{(100 - 18)\ \mathrm{^\circ C}}{\mathrm{0.35\ ^\circ C/s}} = \mathrm{234\ s} $$

    1. Analyze the data by drawing a scatter plot with a best-fit line and calculating the model equation.

      2. Graph

      The \(y\)-intercept can be read from the graph: \(b\) = 81.1 kg.

      Using the points (0 days, 81.1 kg) and (30 days, 78.5 kg), the slope is: $$k = \frac{m_2 - m_1}{\Delta t} = \mathrm{\frac{(78.5 - 81.1)\ kg}{30\ days} = -0.087\ kg/day}$$ The model equation (i.e. the equation of the best-fit line) is: $$m = (\mathrm{–0.087\ kg/day})\ t + \mathrm{81.1\ kg}$$

    2. Interpret your results for the slope and the y-intercept. In other words, what do these values tell you about Kelly’s weight-loss progress?
      3. Kelly had a starting mass of 81.1 kg and lost 0.087 kg per day on average.
    3. Estimate how long it should take Kelly to reach his goal of 75.0 kg. What assumptions are you making?
      4. Rearrange the model equation to isolate \(t\)... $$m - \mathrm{81.1\ kg} = (\mathrm{–0.087\ kg/day})\ t$$ $$t = \frac{m - \mathrm{81.1\ kg}}{\mathrm{–0.087\ kg/day}}$$ Now evaluate with \(m\) = 75.0 kg... $$t = \frac{\mathrm{(75.0 - 81.1)\ kg}}{\mathrm{–0.087\ kg/day}} = \mathrm{70\ days}$$
    4. Estimate what Kelly’s mass will be after 60 days. What assumptions are you making? Are these assumptions realistic? Explain.
      5. $$m = \mathrm{(–0.087\ kg/day)\ 60\ days + 81.1\ kg = 75.9\ kg}$$ We are assuming that the weight loss continues at the same rate until at least day 60.
