    3. Calculate the slope of the best-fit line.
      4. Using the points (0.00 m, 96.9 kPa) and (8.00 m, 175.7 kPa) the slope is: $$k = \mathrm{\frac{(175.7-96.9)\ kPa}{(8.00 - 0.00)\ m} = 9.85\ kPa/m}$$
    4. What is the significance of the slope?
      5. A slope of 9.85 kPa/m means that the pressure increases by 9.85 kilopascals for every increase in depth of 1.0 metre.
    5. Estimate how deep the divers would need to go to encounter water pressure of 300 kPa. Explain how you obtained your answer. Is this an interpolation or extrapolation?
      6. 300 kPa is 203.1 kPa more than at a depth of zero (where it is 96.9 kPa), so this would occur at an approximate depth of: $$\mathrm{\frac{203.1\ kPa}{9.85\ kPa/m} = 20.6\ m}$$

      4. Using the points (5, 64%) and (19, 50%) the slope is: $$k = \mathrm{\frac{(50 - 64)\%}{19 - 5} = -1.0\%}$$

      5. This means that based on this data a student’s mark drops by 1.0% for each additional absence.

      6. The line crosses the y-axis near 69.5%. This is the expected mark for an average student with zero absences.
    6. Why are the data points so scattered?
      7. There are many other independent variables besides absences that were not controlled for. This often causes the data to become more scattered than in a properly controlled experiment.
