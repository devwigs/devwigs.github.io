Practice

  1. Convert these measurements to standard SI units.
    1. 32.5 grams
    2. 625 μs
    3. 6.40 cm3
    4. 80.0 km/h
    $$\require{cancel}\mathrm{32.5\ g = 3.25\times 10^1 \cancel{g}\ \color{blue}{\frac{1\ kg}{10^3 \cancel{g}}} = 3.25\times 10^{-2}\ kg}$$ $$\mathrm{625\ \mu s = 6.25\times 10^2 \cancel{\mu s} \color{blue}{\frac{10^{-6}\ s}{1 \cancel{\mu s}}} = 6.25\times 10^{-4}\ s}$$ $$\mathrm{6.40\ cm^3 = 6.40 \cancel{cm^3} \color{blue}{\left(\frac{10^{-2}\ m}{1\ \cancel{cm}}\right)^3} = 6.40\times 10^{-6}\ m^3}$$ $$\mathrm{80.0\ \frac{km}{h} = 80.0\ \frac{\cancel{km}}{\cancel{h}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^3\ m}{1\ \cancel{km}} \cdot \frac{1\ \cancel{h}}{3600\ s}} = 22.2\ m/s}$$
  2. Convert these measurements to the specified unit.
    1. 0.0718 kg [convert to grams]
    2. 0.925 mA [convert to μA]
    3. 8.5 m/s [convert to km/h]
    4. 3.52 g/cm3 [convert to kg/m3]
    3. $$\mathrm{0.0718\ kg = 7.18\times 10^{-2}\ \cancel{kg} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^3\ g}{1 \cancel{kg}}} = 71.8\ g}$$ $$\mathrm{0.925\ mA = 9.25\times 10^{-1}\ \cancel{mA} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{10^{-3} \cancel{A}}{1\ \cancel{mA}}\cdot \frac{1\ \mu A}{10^{-6}\ \cancel{A}}} = 925\ \mu A}$$ $$\mathrm{8.5\ m/s = 8.5\ \frac{\cancel{m}}{\cancel{s}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{1\ km}{10^3 \cancel{m}} \cdot\frac{3600 \cancel{s}}{1\ h}} = 31\ km/h}$$ $$\mathrm{3.52\ g/cm^3 = 3.52\ \frac{\cancel{g}}{\cancel{cm^3}} \color{blue}{\cdot\frac{1\ kg}{10^3 \cancel{g}} \left(\frac{\cancel{cm}}{10^{-2}\ m}\right)^3} = 3.52\times 10^3\ kg/m^3}$$
  3. Earth’s atmosphere has a “standard” air pressure of 14.7 psi (pounds per square inch). Express this measurement using the standard SI unit, N/m2 (also called pascals, Pa). The conversion factors are 1 inch = 0.0254 m and 1 pound = 4.45 newtons.
    4. $$\mathrm{14.7\ \frac{lb}{in^2} = 14.7\ \frac{\cancel{lb}}{\cancel{in}^2} \color{blue}{\cdot \frac{4.45\ N}{1\ \cancel{lb}} \left(\frac{1\ \cancel{in}}{0.0254\ m}\right)^2} = 1.01\times 10^5\ Pa}$$
