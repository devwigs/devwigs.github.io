Problems

    1. Sketch a diagram of the gravitational field surrounding a planet of mass 8.25 × 1024 kg.
    2. Calculate the gravitational field strength of the planet at a separation of 1.00 × 108 m.
      3. $$\begin{align}g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2} \cdot 8.25\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(1.00\times 10^8\ m)^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{0.0550\ m/s^2}\end{align}$$
    3. Calculate the gravitational field strength of the planet at a separation of 5.00 × 108 m.
      4. $$\begin{align}g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2} \cdot 8.25\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(5.00\times 10^8\ m)^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{0.00220\ m/s^2}\end{align}$$

  2. A binary star system contains a “primary” star of mass 5.00 × 1030 kg, located at the origin, and a secondary star of mass 1.50 × 1030 kg, located at coordinates (80.0, 0.00) au. A spacecraft is located at coordinates (70.0, 20.0) au. The scale of the diagram is 1 square = 10 au.

    1. Solve for all remaining sides and angles of the triangles on the diagram. Show all work!
      2. $$\alpha = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{20}{70}\right) = 15.9^\circ$$ $$\beta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{20}{10}\right) = 63.4^\circ$$ $$r_P = \mathrm{\sqrt{70^2 + 20^2}\ au = 72.8\ au = 1.09\times 10^{13}\ m}$$ $$r_S = \mathrm{\sqrt{10^2 + 20^2}\ au = 22.4\ au = 3.35\times 10^{12}\ m}$$
    2. Calculate the gravitational field (magnitude and direction) at the spacecraft’s location due to the primary star. Illustrate the vector \({\vec{\mathbf{g}}}_P\) on the diagram above. Remember to convert au to metres!
      3. $$\begin{align}g_P &= \frac{GM_P}{r_P^2} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2} \cdot 5.00\times 10^{30}\ kg}{(1.09\times 10^{13}\ m)^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{2.81\times 10^{-6}\ m/s^2}\end{align}$$
    3. Calculate the gravitational field at the spacecraft’s location due to the secondary star. Illustrate the vector \({\vec{\mathbf{g}}}_S\) on the diagram above.
      4. $$\begin{align}g_S &= \frac{GM_S}{r_S^2} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2} \cdot 1.50\times 10^{30}\ kg}{(3.35\times 10^{12}\ m)^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{8.94\times 10^{-6}\ m/s^2}\end{align}$$
    4. Calculate the net gravitational field at the spacecraft’s location due to the combined field of both stars. Show your work below the table and express the net field in polar form.
