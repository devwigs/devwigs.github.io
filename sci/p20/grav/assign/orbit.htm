Practice

    1. Use the mass of Earth to calculate the orbital radius of a satellite in geosynchronous orbit around the Earth. Compare this to the answer you calculated in #4c of the Planetary Motion (Kepler’s 3rd Law) assignment.
      2. $$\frac{GM}{r^2} = g = a_c = \frac{4\pi^2r}{T^2}$$ $$GMT^2 = 4\pi^2r^3$$ $$r^3 = \frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}$$ $$r = \left(\frac{GMT^2}{4\pi^2}\right)^{1/3}$$ $$r = \mathrm{\left(\frac{(6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}) (5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg) (8.64\times 10^4\ s)^2}{4\pi^2}\right)^{1/3}}$$ $$r = \mathrm{4.22\times 10^7\ m}$$
    2. Calculate the speed of the satellite and the gravitational field strength that it experiences.
      3. $$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 4.22\times 10^7\ m}{8.64\times 10^4\ s} = 3.07\times 10^3\ m/s}$$ $$\begin{align}\ \\g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\\\ &= \mathrm{\frac{(6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}) (5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg)}{(4.22\times 10^7\ m)^2}} \\\\ &= \mathrm{0.223 m/s^2}\end{align}$$
    1. What speed does a space shuttle need to have to orbit the Earth at an altitude of 170 km above the ground? [Remember that \(r\) must be measured from the Earth’s centre, not the ground!]
      2. $$r = \mathrm{6.37\times 10^6\ m + 1.70\times 10^5\ m = 6.54\times 10^6\ m}$$ $$\frac{GM}{r^2} = g = a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$ $$\frac{GM}{r} = v^2$$ $$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$ $$v = \mathrm{\sqrt{\frac{(6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2})(5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg)}{6.54\times 10^6\ m}}} = \mathrm{7.80\times 10^3\ m/s}$$
    2. What would the period of this orbit be?
      3. $$v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}$$ $$vT = 2\pi r$$ $$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 6.54\times 10^6\ m}{7.80\times 10^3\ m/s} = 5.27\times 10^3\ s}$$
    3. What would the space shuttle’s centripetal acceleration be?
      4. $$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \mathrm{\frac{(7.80\times 10^3\ m/s)^2}{6.54\times 10^6\ m} = 9.31\ m/s^2}$$
    4. How strong is Earth’s gravitational field at this altitude?
      5. 9.31 m/s2!
  3. A comet with a speed of 2.45 km/s is captured by the gravity of a planet that has a mass of 3.16 × 1025 kg. Determine the orbital radius and period of the comet assuming uniform circular motion.
    4. $$\frac{GM}{r^2} = g = a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$ $$GM = v^2r$$ $$r = \frac{GM}{v^2} = \mathrm{\frac{(6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2})(3.16\times 10^{25}\ kg)}{(2.45\times 10^3\ m/s)^2} = 3.51\times 10^8\ m}$$ For \(T\), the algebra is the same as #2b... $$T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 3.51\times 10^8\ m}{2.45\times 10^3\ m/s} = 9.01\times 10^5\ s}$$

  5. A research satellite orbits a newly discovered planet. The satellite has an orbital radius of 2.85 × 107 m and a period of 3.20 h. Calculate the mass of the planet.
    6. $$\frac{GM}{r^2} = g = a_c = \frac{4\pi^2r}{T^2}$$ $$GM = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{T^2}$$ $$M = \frac{4\pi^2r^3}{GT^2} = \mathrm{\frac{4\pi^2 (2.85\times 10^7\ m)^3}{(6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2})(1.15\times 10^4\ s)^2} = 1.03\times 10^{26}\ kg}$$
