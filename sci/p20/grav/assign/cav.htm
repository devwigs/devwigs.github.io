Substitute the model equation into the Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation equation: $$F_g = Gm_1m_2 r^{-2} = \mathrm{(1.74\times 10^{-11}\ N\cdot m^2)}\ r^{-2}$$ $$Gm_1m_2 = \mathrm{1.74\times 10^{-11}\ N\cdot m^2}$$ $$\begin{align} G &= \frac{\mathrm{1.74\times 10^{-11}\ N\cdot m^2}}{m_1m_2} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{1.74\times 10^{-11}\ N\cdot m^2}{3.50\ kg\cdot 0.0750\ kg}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{6.63\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}} \end{align}$$

    1. Cavendish was a geologist by training and was interested in determining the mass of the Earth. Using the value of \(G\) determined from the analysis of the data above and using Earth’s surface gravity of 9.81 m/s2 and radius of 6.37 × 106 m, calculate the mass of the Earth.
      2. $$\begin{align} g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\ \\ g r^2 &= GM \\ \\ M &= \frac{g r^2}{G} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{9.81 m/s^2 \cdot (6.37\times 10^6\ m)^2}{6.63\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{6.00\times 10^{24}\ kg} \end{align}$$
