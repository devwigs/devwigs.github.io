Practice

  1. A satellite orbiting the Earth experiences a centripetal acceleration of 7.06 m/s2 due to Earth’s gravity.
    1. How far is the satellite from the Earth’s centre?
      2. $$\begin{align} g &= \frac{GM_{earth}}{r^2} \\ \\ g r^2 &= GM_{earth} \\ \\ r^2 &= \frac{GM_{earth}}{g} \\ \\ r &= \sqrt{\frac{GM_{earth}}{g}} \\ \\ &= \sqrt{\mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg}{7.06\ m/s^2}}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{7.51\times 10^6\ m} \end{align}$$
    2. What is the satellite’s altitude?
      3. $$\mathrm{7.51\times 10^6\ m - 6.37\times 10^6\ m = 1.14\times 10^6\ m}$$
  2. Calculate the acceleration due to Earth’s gravitational at an altitude of 275 km above the ground.
    3. $$r = \mathrm{6.37\times 10^6\ m + 2.75\times 10^5\ m = 6.65\times 10^6\ m}$$ $$g = \frac{GM_{earth}}{r^2} = \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(6.65\times 10^6\ m)^2}} = \mathrm{9.00\ m/s^2}$$
  3. What is the mass of a star if a planet orbiting at a radius of 4.86 × 1011 m experiences a gravitational acceleration of 1.20 × 10–3 m/s2?
    4. $$\begin{align} g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\ \\ gr^2 &= GM \\ \\ M &= \frac{gr^2}{G} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{1.20\times 10^{-3}\ m/s^2 (4.86\times 10^{11}\ m)^2}{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{4.25\times 10^{30}\ kg} \end{align}$$
  4. How far apart are two identical 1350 kg cars if they attract each other with a force of 8.44 × 10–9 N? Why would we not notice this gravitational attraction in everyday life?
    5. $$\begin{align} F_g &= \frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2} \\ \\ F_g r^2 &= Gm_1m_2 \\ \\ r^2 &= \frac{Gm_1m_2}{F_g} \\ \\ r &= \sqrt{\frac{Gm_1m_2}{F_g}} \\ \\ &= \sqrt{\mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot (1350\ kg)^2}{8.44\times 10^{-9}\ N}}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{120\ m} \end{align}$$
    1. Two asteroids are located 1.28 km apart. The asteroids experience a gravitational attraction of 40.7 N. If the smaller asteroid’s mass is 3.51 × 108 kg, what is the mass of the larger asteroid?
      2. $$\begin{align} F_g &= \frac{Gm_1m_2}{r^2} \\ \\ F_g r^2 &= Gm_1m_2 \\ \\ m_2 &= \frac{F_g r^2}{Gm_1} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{40.7\ N\ (1.28\times 10^3\ m)^2}{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2} \cdot 3.51\times 10^8\ kg}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{2.85\times 10^9\ kg} \end{align}$$
    2. Calculate the acceleration of each asteroid.
      3. $$a_1 = \frac{F_g}{m_1} = \mathrm{\frac{40.7\ N}{3.51\times 10^8\ kg} = 1.16\times 10^{-7}\ m/s^2}$$ $$a_2 = \frac{F_g}{m_2} = \mathrm{\frac{40.7\ N}{2.85\times 10^9\ kg} = 1.43\times 10^{-8}\ m/s^2}$$
    3. Why does the smaller one accelerate at a greater rate than the larger one?
      4. Each asteroid experiences the same force; therefore the more massive asteroid accelerates at a slower rate.
  6. A satellite orbiting a planet with a radius \(r\) experiences a gravitational acceleration of 5.60 m/s2. What gravitational acceleration will a satellite orbiting at \(2r\) experience?

    7. Because the separation is double, the gravity is four times weaker due to the inverse square law: $$g_2 = \mathrm{\frac{5.60\ m/s^2}{4} = 1.40\ m/s^2}$$

    We can show this algebraically: $$g_1 = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$ $$g_2 = \frac{GM}{(2r)^2} = \frac{GM}{4r^2} = \frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{GM}{r^2}\right) = \frac{1}{4} g_1$$

  7. A 500 kg spacecraft is at the midpoint between the Earth and the Moon. What net gravitational force and acceleration does the spacecraft experience? [Hint: How far is the spacecraft from Earth’s centre? From the Moon’s centre?]

    8. Since the spacecraft is at the midpoint, its separation from both Earth and the Moon is half of the Earth-Moon separation: $$r = \mathrm{\frac{3.84\times 10^8\ m}{2} = 1.92\times 10^8\ m}$$

    Use the universal gravitation equation to calculate the gravity produced by each source on the spacecraft: $$g_E = \frac{GM_E}{r^2} = \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 5.97\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(1.92\times 10^8\ m)^2} = 0.0108\ m/s^2}$$ $$g_M = \frac{GM_M}{r^2} = \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 7.40\times 10^{22}\ kg}{(1.92\times 10^8\ m)^2} = 1.34\times 10^{-4}\ m/s^2}$$

    Keep in mind that the forces are in opposite directions. Using + as “toward the Moon” and – as “toward the Earth” , the net gravity is: $$\vec{\mathbf g} = \vec{\mathbf g}_E + \vec{\mathbf g}_M = \mathrm{-0.0108\ m/s^2 + 1.34\times 10^{-4}\ m/s^2 = -0.0107\ m/s^2}$$

    The net force is: $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf g} = \mathrm{500\ kg (-0.0107\ m/s^2) = -5.33\ N}$$

  8. Calculate the acceleration due to gravity (\(g\)) on the surface of a planet with a radius of 7250 km and a mass of 8.92 × 1024 kg.
    9. $$\begin{align} g &= \frac{GM}{r^2} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 8.92\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(7.25\times 10^6\ m)^2}} \\ \\ &= \mathrm{11.3\ m/s^2} \end{align}$$
    1. The planet described in the previous question has a moon with a mass of 4.25 × 1022 kg that orbits at a radius of 2.54 × 108 m. Determine the acceleration due to gravity produced at the midpoint between the planet and the moon due to (i) the planet alone and (ii) the moon alone.
      2. $$r = \mathrm{\frac{2.54\times 10^8\ m}{2} = 1.27\times 10^8\ m}$$

      $$g_P = \frac{GM_P}{r^2} = \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 8.92\times 10^{24}\ kg}{(1.27\times 10^8\ m)^2} = 0.0369\ m/s^2}$$ $$g_M = \frac{GM_M}{r^2} = \mathrm{\frac{6.67\times 10^{-11}\ \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\cdot 4.25\times 10^{22}\ kg}{(1.27\times 10^8\ m)^2} = 1.76\times 10^{-4}\ m/s^2}$$

    2. Determine the net gravitational field (the vector total of the gravitational fields due to the two sources) at that point.
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf g} = \vec{\mathbf g}_P + \vec{\mathbf g}_M = \mathrm{-0.0369\ m/s^2 + 1.76\times 10^{-4}\ m/s^2 = -0.0367\ m/s^2}$$
