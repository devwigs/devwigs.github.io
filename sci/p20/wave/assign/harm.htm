Practice

    1. Draw a graph (on graph paper using an accurate scale) of a harmonic wave moving along a string with an amplitude of 2.00 cm and a wavelength of 5.00 cm.
      2. Graph
    2. Calculate the period and frequency of the wave if its speed is 80.0 cm/s.
      3. $$v = \lambda f$$ $$f = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \mathrm{\frac{0.800\ m/s}{0.0500\ m} = 16.0\ Hz}$$ $$T = \frac{1}{f} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{16.0\ Hz} = 0.0625\ s}$$
    3. How far does the wave travel in 12.5 milliseconds?
      4. $$\Delta d = v\Delta t = \mathrm{0.800\ m/s\cdot 0.0125\ s = 0.0100\ m}$$
    4. On the graph from (a), sketch the wave after 12.5 milliseconds has passed. Assume the wave is moving to the right.
    5. Is the wave transverse or longitudinal? Explain.
      6. Waves moving along a string are transverse.
    6. Determine the direction of motion (up or down) of the string for (i) the leading half of the crests, (ii) the trailing half of the crests, (iii) the leading half of the troughs, and (iv) the trailing half of the troughs.
      7. i & iv: up; ii & iii: down
    1. As a water wave passes along the surface of a lake, students in a boat notice that the boat executes SHM with a period of 1.80 s. They measure the wavelength of the surface waves to be 4.25 m. What is the speed of the wave?
      2. $$v = \frac{\lambda}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{4.25\ m}{1.80\ s} = 2.36\ m/s}$$
    2. Does this wave have transverse, longitudinal or both components?
      3. Water waves have both.
    1. A tuning fork produces sound waves with a frequency of 220 Hz. If these waves have a wavelength of 151 cm, what is the speed of sound?
      2. $$v = \lambda f = \mathrm{1.51\ m\cdot 220\ Hz = 332\ m/s}$$
    2. Is this wave transverse or longitudinal?
      3. Sound waves are longitudinal.
  4. Electrical appliances often produce radio waves with a frequency of 60.0 Hz. Radio waves travel at the speed of light (3.00 × 108 m/s). What is the wavelength of these waves?
    5. $$v = \lambda f$$ $$\lambda = \frac{v}{f} = \mathrm{\frac{3.00\times 10^8\ m/s}{60.0\ Hz} = 5.00\times 10^7\ m}$$
    1. The human eye can detect light waves with wavelengths between about 390 nm and 750 nm. Determine the range of frequencies detectable by the human eye? ([Use the speed of light waves given in the previous question.]
      2. $$f = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \mathrm{\frac{3.00\times 10^8\ m/s}{3.90\times 10^{-7}\ m} = 7.69\times 10^{14}\ Hz}$$ $$f = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \mathrm{\frac{3.00\times 10^8\ m/s}{7.50\times 10^{-7}\ m} = 4.00\times 10^{14}\ Hz}$$
    2. Visible light waves may be produced by electrons within molecules. Estimate the period of SHM for an electron that emits light at 390 nm.
      3. $$T = \frac{1}{f} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{7.69\times 10^{14}\ Hz} = 1.30\times 10^{-15}\ s}$$
    3. Estimate the speed of the electron if its amplitude of SHM is 5.29 × 10–11 m.
      4. $$v_{max} = \frac{2\pi A}{T} = \mathrm{\frac{2\pi\cdot 5.29\times 10^{-11}\ m}{1.30\times 10^{-11}\ s} = 2.56\times 10^5\ m/s}$$
  6. The graph below shows a transverse harmonic wave at \(t\) = 0 (bold curve) and again at \(t\) = 0.250 s (lighter curve).

    1. Determine the wavelength and amplitude of the wave.
      2. $$A = \mathrm{3.00\times 10^{-2}\ m}$$ $$\lambda = \mathrm{2.80\ m}$$
    2. Estimate the speed of the wave. [Hint: What distance did the wave travel in 0.250 s?]
      3. $$v = \frac{\Delta d}{\Delta t} = \mathrm{\frac{0.40\ m}{0.250\ s} = 1.6\ m/s}$$
    3. Calculate the period and frequency of the wave.
      4. $$v = \lambda f$$ $$f = \frac{v}{\lambda} = \mathrm{\frac{1.6\ m/s}{2.80\ m} = 0.571\ Hz}$$ $$T = \frac{1}{f} = \mathrm{\frac{1}{0.571\ Hz} = 1.75\ s}$$
    4. In what direction is the wave moving, left or right?
      5. Right
    5. What are the directions of the particle velocities at points A, B and C? At which of these points do the particles have the greatest velocity?

      6. A & B: down; C: up

      Point C is the equilibrium so it has the greatest speed.
