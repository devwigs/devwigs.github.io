  4. As a cyclist is riding toward her friend, both the cyclist and the stationary friend strike identical 480 Hz tuning forks. If they hear 5.0 beats per second, how fast is the cyclist riding?
    The beat pattern occurs because the Doppler Effect causes the observer to hear the sound from the moving source at a higher frequency when the source is moving toward the observer. For a 5.0 Hz beat frequency, \(f_o\) = 480 Hz + 5.0 Hz = 485 Hz. The Doppler formula can be simplified for \(v_o\) = 0: $$f_o = \frac{v - v_o}{v - v_s}\ f_s = \frac{v}{v - v_s}\ f_s$$ $$f_o (v - v_s) = v f_s$$ $$v - v_s = \frac{v f_s}{f_o}$$ $$-v_s = \frac{v f_s}{f_o} - v$$ $$v_s = -\frac{v f_s}{f_o} + v = \mathrm{-\frac{343\ m/s\cdot 480\ Hz}{485\ Hz} + 343\ m/s = 3.54\ m/s}$$
