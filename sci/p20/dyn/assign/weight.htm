Review

  1. Define mass and weight; explain how these differ.
  2. State the relationship between mass and weight.
  3. When an object is moved to a location where gravity is weaker than it is on Earth, how (if at all) do its mass and weight change?
  4. Explain the difference between apparent weight and actual weight.

Practice

  1. Determine the weight on Earth of objects with masses:
    1. 1.00 kg
      2. $$F_g = mg = \mathrm{1.00\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2 = 9.81\ N}$$
    2. 2.00 kg
      3. $$F_g = mg = \mathrm{2.00\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2 = 19.6\ N}$$
    3. 103.2 kg
      4. $$F_g = mg = \mathrm{103.2\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2 = 1.01\times 10^3\ N}$$
    4. 32.4 mg
      5. $$F_g = mg = \mathrm{32.4\times 10^{-6}\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2 = 3.18\times 10^{-4}\ N}$$
  2. Determine the mass of objects that weigh (on Earth):
    1. 1.00 N
      2. $$m = \frac{F_g}{g} = \mathrm{\frac{1.00\ N}{9.81\ m/s^2} = 0.102\ kg}$$
    2. 25.0 N
      3. $$m = \frac{F_g}{g} = \mathrm{\frac{25.0\ N}{9.81\ m/s^2} = 2.55\ kg}$$
    3. 46.8 mN
      4. $$m = \frac{F_g}{g} = \mathrm{\frac{46.8\times 10^{-3}\ N}{9.81\ m/s^2} = 0.00477\ kg}$$
    4. 1.59 × 106 N
      5. $$m = \frac{F_g}{g} = \mathrm{\frac{1.59\times 10^6\ N}{9.81\ m/s^2} = 1.62\times 10^5\ kg}$$
  3. A deluxe cheeseburger weighs 2.30 N on Earth. An astronaut about to eat the burger on an alien planet notices that it weighs 2.97 N.
    1. Calculate the burger’s mass.
      2. $$m = \frac{F_g}{g} = \mathrm{\frac{2.30\ N}{9.81\ m/s^2} = 0.234\ kg}$$
    2. Calculate the magnitude of \(\vec{\mathbf{g}}\) on the alien planet.
      3. $$g = \frac{F_g}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{2.97\ N}{0.234\ kg} = 12.7\ m/s^2}$$
    1. A 78.0 kg skydiver experiences an upward force of 205 N due to air resistance, in addition to the force of gravity. Determine her acceleration.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_g = m\vec{\mathbf g} = \mathrm{78.0\ kg\cdot (-9.81\ m/s^2) = -765\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{-765\ N + 205\ N = -560\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{-560\ N}{78.0\ kg} = -7.18\ m/s^2}$$
    2. As the skydiver accelerates, the air resistance gradually increases. A short time later, the skydiver’s downward acceleration is 5.30 m/s2. What is the air resistance at this point?
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{78.0\ kg\cdot (-5.30\ m/s^2) = -413\ N}$$ $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} &= \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f \\ \vec{\mathbf F}_f &= \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} - \vec{\mathbf F}_g \\ &= \mathrm{-413\ N - (-765\ N)} \\ &= \mathrm{+352\ N} \end{align}$$
  5. Determine the mass of an object that has an acceleration of –6.25 m/s2 as a result of gravity and an upward air resistance of 18.0 N.
    6. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f$$ Substitute \(\vec{\mathbf F}_g = m\vec{\mathbf g}\)... $$\begin{align} m\vec{\mathbf a} &= m\vec{\mathbf g} + \vec{\mathbf F}_f \\ m\vec{\mathbf a} - m\vec{\mathbf g} &= \vec{\mathbf F}_f \\ m (\vec{\mathbf a} - \vec{\mathbf g}) &= \vec{\mathbf F}_f \\ m &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_f}{\vec{\mathbf a} - \vec{\mathbf g}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{18.0\ N}{-6.25\ m/s^2 - (-9.81\ m/s^2)}} \\ &= \mathrm{5.06\ kg} \end{align}$$
  6. A 21.0 g acorn falls 4.00 m in 1.12 s, starting from rest. What force of air resistance did it experience?

    7. Find the acceleration using the uniform accelerated motion equations. Knowing the the initial velocity is zero, we can simplify the equations by ignoring the \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\) terms. (Alternatively, you could find the final velocity first and then the acceleration.) $$\begin{align} \Delta\vec{\mathbf d} &= \frac{1}{2} \vec{\mathbf a} (\Delta t)^2 \\ \vec{\mathbf a} &= \frac{2\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}}{(\Delta t)^2} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{2\cdot(-4.00\ m)}{(1.12\ s)^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{-6.38\ m/s^2} \end{align}$$

    Now calculate the force of gravity and the net force... $$\vec{\mathbf F}_g = m\vec{\mathbf g} = \mathrm{0.0210\ kg\cdot (-9.81\ m/s^2) = -0.206\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{0.0210\ kg\cdot (-6.38\ m/s^2) = -0.134\ N}$$

    Finally find the air resistance... $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} &= \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f \\ \vec{\mathbf F}_f &= \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} - \vec{\mathbf F}_g \\ &= \mathrm{-0.134\ N - (-0.206\ N) = +0.0721\ N} \end{align}$$

  7. A student with a mass of 60.0 kg stands on a scale. As the student jumps off the scale, the scale reading increases to 610 N. This is the student’s apparent weight, which is actually the normal force of the scale pushing on the student.
    1. What was the original reading on the scale (the student’s actual weight)?
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_g = m\vec{\mathbf g} = \mathrm{60.0\ kg\cdot (-9.81\ m/s^2) = -589\ N}$$
    2. What was the student’s vertical acceleration when jumping off the scale?
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_n = \mathrm{-589\ N + 610\ N = +21.4\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+21.4\ N}{60.0\ kg} = +0.357\ m/s^2}$$
