Practice

    1. Draw a free body diagram and a vector diagram, when a falling object experiences gravity of \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_g\) = –63.8 N and air resistance of \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_f\) = +12.5 N.

      2. The scale of the vector diagram (right) is 10 N per tick.

    2. The mass of the object is 6.50 kg. Calculate the net force, and then use your result to calculate acceleration of the mass.
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{-63.8\ N + 12.5\ N = -51.3\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{-51.3\ N}{6.50\ kg} = -7.89\ m/s^2}$$
  2. Calculate the mass of an object that has an acceleration of –3.92 m/s2 when the forces are \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_f\) = +15.0 N for air resistance and \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_g\) = –25.0 N for gravity. The free body diagram is the same as for the previous question. Include a labelled vector diagram drawn to scale as part of your solution.
    3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{-25.0\ N + 15.0\ N = -10.0\ N}$$ $$m = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{-10.0\ N}{-3.92\ m/s^2} = 2.55\ kg}$$
  3. When a skydiver first deploys her parachute, the upward air resistance is stronger than the downward gravity force due to the large surface area of the parachute. A 58.0 kg skydiver experiences gravity of \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_g\) = –567 N and air resistance of \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_f\) = +725 N.
    1. Draw a free body diagram and a vector diagram to scale.

      2. Each tick is 50 N. The blue arrow is the net force.

      Free body diagram is the same as #1 except that the acceleration is directed up.

    2. Calculate the skydiver’s acceleration.
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{-567\ N + 725\ N = +158\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+158\ N}{58.0\ kg} = +2.72\ m/s^2}$$
    3. Explain what it means to have a positive acceleration while falling in the negative direction.
      4. It means that she is slowing down. For example, if her velocity was –30 m/s before she opened her parachute and –10 m/s after the parachute opened, her change in velocity would be positive: \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf v}\) = (–10 m/s) – (–30 m/s) = +20 m/s.

  4. An object being pushed along a horizontal surface has the free body diagram shown below.

    The force applied (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_a\)) by the person pushes the object to the right (+) while the friction force (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_f\)) acts in the opposite (–) direction. According to Newton’s 2nd Law, the acceleration will be in the positive direction when the applied force is stronger than the friction force, and in the negative direction when the friction is stronger.

    The force of gravity pulling down and the upward normal force of the ground pushing on the object will also be present; however, if the object only moves in the horizontal direction, these vertical forces will be perfectly balanced so that we can ignore them when calculating \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_{net}\).

    1. Calculate the friction force when an applied force of +58.9 N causes a 6.00 kg mass to accelerate at +4.00 m/s2.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{6.00\ kg\cdot (+4.00\ m/s^2) = +24.0\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_a + \vec{\mathbf F}_f$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_f = \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} - \vec{\mathbf F}_a = \mathrm{+24.0\ N - 58.9\ N = -34.9\ N}$$
    2. A 2.00 kg mass experiences a friction force of –26.1 N and accelerates at \(-3.25\) m/s2. Calculate the applied force acting on the mass and include a vector diagram.
      3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{2.00\ kg\cdot (-3.25\ m/s^2) = -6.50\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_a + \vec{\mathbf F}_f$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_a = \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} - \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{-6.50\ N - (-26.1\ N) = +19.6\ N}$$
    3. Calculate the mass of the object if it accelerates at +4.76 m/s2 when the applied force is +35.0 N and the friction is –18.0 N. Include a vector diagram.
      4. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_a + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{+35.0\ N - 18.0\ N = +17.0\ N}$$ $$m = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{+17.0\ N}{+4.76\ m/s^2} = 3.57\ kg}$$
  5. During launch, a toy rocket experiences a thrust (applied force) of +185 N, air resistance of –38.2 N, and gravity of –49.1 N. All forces are vertical. If the rocket accelerates at +19.5 m/s2, what is its mass?
    6. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_a + \vec{\mathbf F}_g + \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{+185\ N - 49.1\ N - 38.2\ N = +97.7\ N}$$ $$m = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{+97.7\ N}{+19.5\ m/s^2} = 5.01\ kg}$$
  6. A 1420 kg car accelerates from 50.0 km/h to 80.0 km/h over 108 m along a horizontal road. The air resistance acting on the car is –415 N. Calculate the applied force.

    7. Use the uniform accelerated motion equations to find acceleration. Be sure to change km/h to m/s first... $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{v_f^2 - v_i^2}{2\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\frac{(22.2\ m/s)^2 - (13.9\ m/s)^2}{2\cdot 108\ m} = +1.39\ m/s^2}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{1420\ kg\cdot (+1.39\ m/s^2) = +1978\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \vec{\mathbf F}_a + \vec{\mathbf F}_f$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_a = \vec{\mathbf F}_{net} - \vec{\mathbf F}_f = \mathrm{+1978\ N - (-415\ N) = +2393\ N}$$

  7. An ice hockey puck slowly decelerates due to air resistance and friction.
    1. Draw a free body diagram for the puck, illustrating its direction of motion and the direction of all forces acting on the puck.

    2. If the 376 g puck has an initial velocity of 1.25 m/s and takes 23.2 s to come to rest, determine the average net force it experienced.
      3. Use the uniform accelerated motion equations to find acceleration: $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_f - \vec{\mathbf v}_i}{\Delta t} = \mathrm{\frac{0.00\ m/s - 1.25\ m/s}{23.2\ s} = -0.0539\ m/s^2}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{0.376\ kg\cdot (-0.0539\ m/s^2) = -0.0203\ N}$$
