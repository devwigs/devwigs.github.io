Review

  1. Explain the Law of Inertia, also called Newton’s 1st Law of Motion.
    2. Objects at rest will remain at rest and objects in motion will remain in motion with a constant velocity unless acted upon by an unbalanced force. Once an object is set in motion it does not require a force to keep the object in motion!
    1. State Newton’s 2nd Law of Motion and explain how force and mass are related to motion.
    2. What do we mean by an unbalanced force?
    3. When there is an unbalanced force acting on an object, the object will accelerate accordingto the equation: $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a}$$ The unbalanced force, \(\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\), is the vector sum of all of the forces acting on the object.
    1. Is force a scalar or vector?
      2. Vector; it has a direction.
    2. What is the SI unit of force? How is this unit defined?
      3. A newton is the amount of force needed to make a 1-kilogram mass accelerate at 1 m/s2: $$\mathrm{N = kg\cdot\frac{m}{s^2}}$$
  4. Based on Newton’s Laws of Motion, what are the requirements for (a) uniform motion, and (b) uniform accelerated motion?
    5. Uniform motion occurs when the net (unbalanced) force is exactly zero. Uniform accelerated motion occurs when there is a constant, non-zero unbalanced force.

Practice

  1. An object slides along a horizontal surface. For each scenario, state whether the motion will be uniform or accelerated. If accelerated motion, state whether the object will speed up or slow down.
    1. There is friction acting on the object but no applied force.
    2. There is an applied force that is equal and opposite to friction.
    3. There is an applied force that is opposite and greater than friction.
    4. There are no forces at all (not even friction.)
    1. Uniform Accelerated Motion (Slowing Down)
    2. Uniform Motion
    3. Uniform Accelerated Motion (Speeding Up)
    4. Uniform Motion
  2. How much net force is required to accelerate a 12.5 kg mass at a rate of 2.36 m/s2?
    3. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{12.5\ kg\cdot (+2.36\ m/s^2) = +29.5\ N}$$
  3. What is the acceleration of a 295 kg satellite experiencing an unbalanced force of \(1.25\times 10^3\) N?
    4. $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+1.25\times 10^3\ N}{295\ kg} = +4.24\ m/s^2}$$
  4. An electron accelerates at \(3.51\times 10^{11}\) m/s2 when experiencing a force of \(3.20 \times 10^{–19}\) N. What is the electron’s mass?
    5. $$ m = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} = \mathrm{\frac{+3.20 \times 10^{–19}\ N}{+3.51\times 10^{11}\ m/s^2} = 9.12\times 10^{-31}\ kg}$$
    1. Using the electron mass you calculated in the previous question, determine the acceleration of an electron that experiences a force of \(5.89\times 10^{-19}\) N.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+5.89\times 10^{-19}\ N}{9.12\times 10^{-31}\ kg} = +6.46\times 10^{11}\ m/s^2}$$
    2. The force causes the electron to accelerate from rest to a final speed of \(6.00\times 10^6\) m/s? Calculate (i) the time over which the force acted and (ii) the electron’s displacement while accelerating.
      3. $$\begin{align} \Delta t &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_f - \vec{\mathbf v}_f}{\vec{\mathbf a}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{+6.00\times 10^6\ m/s- 0.00\ m/s}{+6.46\times 10^{11}\ m/s^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{9.29\times 10^{-6}\ s} \\ \\ \Delta\vec{\mathbf d} &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_i + \vec{\mathbf v}_f}{2} \Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{\left(\frac{0.00\ m/s + 6.00\times 10^6\ m/s}{2}\right) 9.29\times 10^{-6}\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{27.9\ m} \end{align}$$
  6. An 82.0 kg runner starting from rest reaches a top speed of 10.6 m/s, 15.2 m from the starting line. Calculate the average net force responsible for accelerating the runner.
    7. $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf a} &= \frac{v_f^2 - v_i^2}{2\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{(10.6\ m/s)^2 - (0.00\ m/s)^2}{2\cdot 15.2\ m}} \\ &= \mathrm{+3.70\ m/s^2} \\ \\\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} &= m\vec{\mathbf a} = \mathrm{82.0\ kg\cdot (+3.70\ m/s^2) = 303\ N} \end{align}$$
  7. A 68.5 kg skydiver experiences a force of 672 N “down” due to gravity and a force of 138 N “up” due to air resistance. What is the skydiver’s acceleration? Include a direction.
    8. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \mathrm{+138\ N + (-672\ N) = -534\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{-534\ N}{68.5\ kg} = -7.80\ m/s^2}$$
