  1. A student pulls a 12.8 kg wagon along a horizontal surface using an applied force of 26.2 N [25.0°]. Other forces acting on the wagon are: air resistance and other forms of friction (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_f\) = 1.65 N [180°]), gravity (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_g\) = 126 N [270°]) and the normal force of the ground pushing up on the wagon’s wheels (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_n\), has a direction of 90° and an unknown magnitude).
    1. Draw a free body diagram showing all forces acting on the wagon and express all forces in Cartesian form.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{ax} = \mathrm{26.2\ N \cos(25.0^\circ) = +23.7\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{ay} = \mathrm{26.2\ N \sin(25.0^\circ) = +11.1\ N}$$
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_a\)+23.7+11.1
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_g\)0–126
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_n\)0?
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_f\)–1.650
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\)?0
    2. Find the normal force by solving the \(y\)-component of Newton’s 2nd Law.
      3. The normal force must balance the other two \(y\)-forces: $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{ny} = \mathrm{0\ N - (-126 + 11.1)\ N = +114.9\ N}$$
    3. Find the acceleration by solving the \(x\)-component of Newton’s 2nd Law.
      4. $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{net} = \mathrm{+23.7\ N + (-1.65\ N) = +22.1\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf a} = \frac{\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}}{m} = \mathrm{\frac{+22.1\ N}{12.8\ kg} = +1.73\ m/s^2}$$
    4. Calculate the speed of the wagon after it has moved 3.00 metres. Assume it started from rest.
      5. $$\begin{align} v_f &= \sqrt{v_i^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\ &= \mathrm{\sqrt{0 + 2(+1.73\ m/s^2)(+3.00\ m)}} \\ &= \mathrm{3.22\ m/s} \end{align}$$
  2. A 50.0-kilogram sign is designed to hang from two cables at 45.0° angles from the top corners as illustrated.

    1. Under normal circumstances (e.g. no strong winds, etc.) how much force does each of the cables exert?

      2. Draw a vector diagram of the three forces. The net force must be zero because the sign is not accelerating. (The scale is 1 square = 40 newtons.)

      $$F_g = mg = \mathrm{50.0\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2 = 491\ N}$$ $$F_1 = F_2 = F_g\cos(45.0^\circ) = \mathrm{347\ N}$$

    2. Suppose that the sign was mounted incorrectly so that the cable on the right makes an angle of 60.0° from horizontal instead of 45.0°. What would the forces in the cables be? Assume that the left cable remains attached at the correct angle.

      3. Draw a new vector diagram with the new angles:

      The easy way to find the forces (if you have taken Math 20) is to use the Law of Sines: $$F_1 = \frac{F_g}{\sin(105^\circ)} \sin(45.0^\circ) = \mathrm{359\ N}$$

      $$F_2 = \frac{F_g}{\sin(105^\circ)} \sin(30.0^\circ) = \mathrm{254\ N}$$

      Without using the Sine Law, we have to use components:

      \(x\)\(y\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_g\)0–491
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_1\)\(F_1\cos(60^\circ)\)
      \(= 0.500F_1\)      		\(F_1\sin(60^\circ)\)
      \(= 0.866F_1\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_2\)\(F_2\cos(135^\circ)\)
      \(= -0.707F_2\)      		\(F_2\sin(135^\circ)\)
      \(= 0.707F_2\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\)00

      The \(x\)-equation is: $$0.500 F_1 - 0.707 F_2 = 0$$ $$0.707 F_2 = 0.500 F_1$$ The \(y\)-equation is: $$0.866 F_1 + 0.707 F_2 - \mathrm{491\ N} = 0$$ We can eliminate \(0.707 F_2\) by substituting the \(x\)-equation into the \(y\)... $$0.866 F_1 + 0.500 F_1 - \mathrm{491\ N} = 0$$ $$1.366 F_1 = \mathrm{491\ N}$$ $$F_1 = \mathrm{\frac{491\ N}{1.366} = 359\ N}$$ We can now use the \(x\)-equation to find the other force... $$F_2 = \frac{0.500 F_1}{0.707} = \mathrm{254\ N}$$

  3. A 1.25 kg physics textbook slides from rest down a 3.00 m long ramp that is inclined at a 15.0° angle. When the book reaches the bottom of the ramp, its speed is found to be 2.42 m/s.

    1. Calculate the acceleration of the book using kinematics and use the result to calculate the net force. If you take the direction parallel to the ramp as the \(x\)-axis, you can solve the problem as a 1D uniform accelerated motion situation since there is no motion in the \(y\)-direction.
    2. Express the force of gravity in Cartesian form. Since we are taking the \(x\)-axis as the direction parallel to the ramp, the direction of gravity is not 270° in this coordinate system.
    3. Find the normal force by solving the \(y\)-component (perpendicular to the ramp) of Newton’s 2nd Law.
    4. Calculate the force of friction from the net force you obtained in (a). Use the answer to calculate the coefficient of kinetic friction.
