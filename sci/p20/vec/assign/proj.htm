Review

  1. Define projectile motion, including

    2. the cause of the motion,
    3. how to analyze the motion.

Practice

  1. An object is thrown horizontally (i.e. with a direction of 0°) with a speed of 8.00 m/s from the top of a 12.5 m tall building. A Physics 20 students made a table of the \(x\)- and \(y\)-components of all kinematics vectors and filled in the known quantities, as shown below. Note that since the acceleration has no \(x\)-component, this means that the initial and final \(x\)-velocities are the same. Also note that time is a scalar, so it does not have separate \(x\)- and \(y\)-components.
     \(x\)\(y\)
    \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s8.000.00
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m–12.5
    \(\Delta t\)s
    1. Illustrate the motion of the object. Show the object’s trajectory and label the vectors: \(\vec{\mathbf a}\), \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\), \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\), \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\) and \(\Delta t\).
    2. Recall from when we studied 1D uniform accelerated motion, we need to know three of the five variables before we can solve for the missing two. Since we know three of the \(y\)-variables, solve for the two missing \(y\)-variables (i.e. the final \(y\)-velocity and time).
      3. $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} &= -\sqrt{v_{iy}^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\ &= \mathrm{-\sqrt{(8.00\ m/s)^2 + 2(-9.81\ m/s^2)(-12.5\ m)}} \\ &= \mathrm{-15.7\ m/s} \\ \\ \Delta t &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} - \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{-15.7\ m/s - 0.00\ m/s}{-9.81\ m/s^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{1.60\ s} \end{align}$$
    3. Using the time you just calculated, solve for the \(x\)-motion.
      4. $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x = \vec{\mathbf v}_x \Delta t = \mathrm{8.00\ m/s\cdot 1.60\ s = 12.8\ m}$$
    4. Express the final velocity vector in polar form.
      5. $$|\vec{\mathbf v}_f| = \mathrm{\sqrt{8.00^2 + (-15.7)^2}\ m/s = 17.6\ m/s}$$ $$\theta = \mathrm{\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{-15.7}{8.00}\right) = -62.9^\circ}$$
  2. A cannonball is shot from the top of a 15.0 m tall castle, with a launch velocity of 35.3 m/s [50.0°].
    1. Express the initial velocity in Cartesian form.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf v}_x = |\vec{\mathbf v}_i| \cos\theta = \mathrm{35.3\ m/s\cdot\cos(50.0^\circ) = 22.7\ m/s}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf v}_{iy} = |\vec{\mathbf v}_i| \sin\theta = \mathrm{35.3\ m/s\cdot\sin(50.0^\circ) = 27.0\ m/s}$$
    2. Calculate the hang time and range (\(x\)-displacement) of the ball.
      3.  \(x\)\(y\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s22.727.0
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s
      \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m–15.0
      \(\Delta t\)s
      $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} &= -\sqrt{v_{iy}^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\ &= \mathrm{-\sqrt{(27.0\ m/s)^2 + 2(-9.81\ m/s^2)(-15.0\ m)}} \\ &= \mathrm{-32.0\ m/s} \\ \\ \Delta t &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} - \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{-32.0\ m/s - 27.0\ m/s}{-9.81\ m/s^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{6.01\ s} \\ \\ \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x &= \vec{\mathbf v}_x \Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{22.7\ m/s\cdot 6.01\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{136\ m} \end{align}$$
    3. Calculate the maximum height the ball reaches. You will need to make a new table since the “final” position is different than in (b).
      4.  \(x\)\(y\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s22.727.0
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s0.00
      \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m
      \(\Delta t\)s
      $$\begin{align} \Delta t &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_f - \vec{\mathbf v}_i}{\vec{\mathbf a}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{0.00\ m/s - 27.0\ m/s}{-9.81\ m/s^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{2.76\ s} \\ \\ \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_i + \vec{\mathbf v}_f}{2}\Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{27.0\ m/s + 0.00\ m/s}{2} 2.76\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{37.2\ m} \end{align}$$

      Note that this is 37.2 m above the initial height of 15.0 m, for a total height of 52.2 m above the ground!

  3. An object thrown from the ground reaches its maximum height in 1.75 s, at which point its horizontal displacement is 7.00 m. Determine the object’s initial velocity and the maximum height it reaches.
    4.  \(x\)\(y\)
    \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s0.00
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m7.00
    \(\Delta t\)s1.75
    $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf v}_x &= \frac{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x}{\Delta t} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{7.00\ m}{1.75\ s}} \\ &= \mathrm{4.00\ m/s} \\ \\ \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy} &= \vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} - \vec{\mathbf a}\Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{0.00\ m/s - (-9.81\ m/s^2)\ 1.75\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{17.2\ m/s} \\ \\ \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_i + \vec{\mathbf v}_f}{2}\Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{17.2\ m/s + 0.00\ m/s}{2} 1.75\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{15.0\ m} \end{align}$$
  4. A golfer drives the ball with an initial velocity of 42.5 m/s [52.4°]. The ball lands on top of a hill that is 2.50 metres higher than the ground from where the shot was made.
    1. Express the initial velocity in Cartesian form.
      2. $$\vec{\mathbf v}_x = |\vec{\mathbf v}_i| \cos\theta = \mathrm{42.5\ m/s\cdot\cos(52.4^\circ) = 25.9\ m/s}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf v}_{iy} = |\vec{\mathbf v}_i| \sin\theta = \mathrm{42.5\ m/s\cdot\sin(52.4^\circ) = 33.7\ m/s}$$
    2. Determine the hang time and range of the ball.
      3.  \(x\)\(y\)
      \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s25.933.7
      \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s
      \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m2.50
      \(\Delta t\)s
      $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} &= -\sqrt{v_{iy}^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} \\ &= \mathrm{-\sqrt{(33.7\ m/s)^2 + 2(-9.81\ m/s^2)(2.50\ m)}} \\ &= \mathrm{-32.9\ m/s} \\ \\ \Delta t &= \frac{\vec{\mathbf v}_{fy} - \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy}}{\vec{\mathbf a}} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{-32.9\ m/s - 33.7\ m/s}{-9.81\ m/s^2}} \\ &= \mathrm{6.79\ s} \\ \\ \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x &= \vec{\mathbf v}_x \Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{25.9\ m/s\cdot 6.79\ s} \\ &= \mathrm{176\ m} \end{align}$$
  5. During a football game a kicker punts the football 80.0 m. The football’s hang time is 5.25 s. Determine the velocity (magnitude and direction!) of the football as it leaves the kicker’s foot.
    6.  \(x\)\(y\)
    \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m80.00.00
    \(\Delta t\)s5.25
    $$\begin{align} \vec{\mathbf v}_x &= \frac{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x}{\Delta t} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{80.0\ m}{5.25\ s}} \\ &= \mathrm{15.2\ m/s} \\ \\ \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy} &= \frac{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} - \frac{1}{2}\vec{\mathbf a}_y (\Delta t)^2}{\Delta t} \\ &= \mathrm{\frac{0.00\ m - \frac{1}{2}(-9.81\ m/s^2) (5.25\ s)^2}{5.25\ s}} \\ &= \mathrm{25.8\ m/s} \\ \\ \\|\vec{\mathbf v}_i| &= \mathrm{\sqrt{15.2^2 + 25.8^2}\ m/s = 29.9\ m/s} \\\theta &= \mathrm{\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{25.8}{15.2}\right) = 59.5^\circ} \end{align}$$
  6. A basketball player releases the ball from a point 2.25 m above the ground and 4.00 m in front of the basket. The basket itself is 3.50 m above the ground. If the ball is released so that its initial direction is 70.0º above the horizontal, what should the ball’s initial speed be so that it lands in the basket? Ignore the effects of air resistance.
    7.  \(x\)\(y\)
    \(\vec{\mathbf a}\)m/s20–9.81
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_i\)m/s\(v_i\cos(70^\circ)\)\(v_i\sin(70^\circ)\)
    \(\vec{\mathbf v}_f\)m/s
    \(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}\)m4.001.25
    \(\Delta t\)s

    For the \(x\)-motion... $$\begin{align} \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x &= \vec{\mathbf v}_x \Delta t \\\mathrm{4.00\ m} &= v_i\cos(70^\circ)\cdot \Delta t \end{align}$$

    For the \(y\)-motion... $$\begin{align} \Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y &= \vec{\mathbf v}_{iy} \Delta t + \frac{1}{2} \vec{\mathbf a}_y (\Delta t)^2 \\\mathrm{1.25\ m} &= v_i\sin(70^\circ)\cdot \Delta t + \frac{1}{2}\vec{\mathbf g}(\Delta t)^2 \end{align}$$

    We can rearrange the \(x\)-equation... $$v_i \Delta t = \frac{\mathrm{4.00\ m}}{\cos(70^\circ)} = \mathrm{11.7\ m}$$

    ...and substitute into the \(y\)-equation... $$\mathrm{1.25\ m} = \mathrm{11.7\ m}\cdot\sin(70^\circ) + \frac{1}{2}\vec{\mathbf g}(\Delta t)^2$$ $$\frac{2}{\vec{\mathbf g}} (\mathrm{1.25\ m} - \mathrm{11.7\ m}\cdot\sin(70^\circ)) = (\Delta t)^2$$

    $$\mathrm{1.99\ s^2} = (\Delta t)^2$$ $$\Delta t = \mathrm{\sqrt{1.99\ s^2} = 1.41\ s}$$

    We’ve already calculated (see above)... $$v_i \Delta t = \mathrm{11.7\ m}$$

    Use the time to find the initial speed... $$v_i = \mathrm{\frac{11.7\ m}{1.41\ s} = 8.30\ m/s}$$
