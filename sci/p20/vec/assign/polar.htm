  1. A hiker undergoes a displacement of (+7.0, –4.0) km.
    1. Convert this vector to polar form using the appropriate trigonometry relations.
    2. Compare your answer to the result you obtain for question 5 of the previous assignment by making measurements from the vector diagram.
    $$\begin{align}|\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}| &= \sqrt{(\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x)^2 + (\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y)^2} \\&= \sqrt{(+7.0)^2 + (-4.0)^2}\mathrm{\ km} \\&= \mathrm{8.1\ km}\end{align}$$ $$\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y}{\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x}\right) = \tan^{-1}\left(\mathrm{\frac{-4.0\ km}{+7.0\ km}}\right) = -29.7^\circ$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{(+7.0, -4.0)\ km = 8.1\ km\ [-29.7^\circ]}$$
  2. A student kicks a soccer ball, causing it to undergo a displacement of 20.0 metres [120°].
    1. Convert this vector to Cartesian form using the appropriate trigonometry relations.
    2. Compare your answer to the result you obtain for question 6 of the previous assignment by making measurements from the vector diagram.
    $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_x = |\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}| \cos\theta = \mathrm{20.0\ m\cdot \cos(120^\circ) = -10.0\ m}$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}_y = |\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}| \sin\theta = \mathrm{20.0\ m\cdot \sin(120^\circ) = +17.3\ m}$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{20.0\ m\ [+120^\circ] = (-10.0, +17.3)\ m}$$

Label the force vector \(({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_a)\), the x- and y-components (\({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_{ax}\), \({\vec{\mathbf{F}}}_{ay}\)) and the direction (\(\theta\)). Take the tail of the vector to be the origin.
    2. Using trigonometric relationships, calculate the magnitude and the direction of the applied force vector.

  5. Calculate the Cartesian components for each vector using trigonometric relationships. Sketch a component diagram for each vector.
    1. \(\vec{\mathbf{p}} = \mathrm{250\ N\cdot s\ [+145^\circ]}\) (The symbol \(\vec{\mathbf{p}}\) represents a “momentum” vector and N·s stands for newton-seconds which is the SI unit of momentum.)

      2. Vector Diagram

      $$\vec{\mathbf p}_x = |\vec{\mathbf p}| \cos\theta = \mathrm{(250\ N\cdot s) \cos(+145^\circ) = -205\ mT}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf p}_y = |\vec{\mathbf p}| \sin\theta = \mathrm{(250\ N\cdot s) \sin(+145^\circ) = +143\ mT}$$
    2. \(\vec{\mathbf{B}} = \mathrm{85.2\ mT\ [-78.2^\circ]}\) (The symbol \(\vec{\mathbf{B}}\) represents a “magnetic field” vector and mT stands for milliteslas, a common unit for measuring magnetic field strength.)

      3. Vector Diagram

      $$\vec{\mathbf B}_x = |\vec{\mathbf B}| \cos\theta = \mathrm{85.2\ mT\cdot \cos(-78.2^\circ) = +17.4\ mT}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf B}_y = |\vec{\mathbf B}| \sin\theta = \mathrm{85.2\ mT\cdot \sin(-78.2^\circ) = -83.4\ mT}$$

  8. A hiker begins at coordinates (3.2, 4.7) km and walks at 4.0 km/h [N 25° W] for 45 minutes.
    1. Determine the hiker’s displacement $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf{d}}=\vec{\mathbf{v}}\Delta t$$ and express it in both polar and Cartesian form.
    2. Draw an accurate, labelled diagram of the motion on graph paper. Identify the coordinates of the hiker’s final position.

    Vector Diagram

    $$\begin{align}\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} &= \vec{\mathbf v}\Delta t \\ &= \mathrm{4.0\ km/h\ [N 25^\circ W]\cdot 0.750\ h} \\ &= \mathrm{3.0\ km\ [115^\circ]} \\ &= \mathrm{(3.0\cos(115^\circ), 3.0\sin(115^\circ))\ km} \\ &= \mathrm{(-1.3, +2.7)\ km} \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align}\vec{\mathbf d}_f &= \vec{\mathbf d}_i + \Delta\vec{\mathbf d} \\ &= \mathrm{(3.2, 4.7)\ km + (-1.3, +2.7)\ km} \\ &= \mathrm{(1.9, 7.4)\ km} \end{align}$$
  9. During its launch, a toy rocket experiences two forces: the force of gravity and the thrust of the rocket engine. The thrust is 52 newtons [+65°] and the force of gravity is 24 N [–90°].
    1. Express both vectors in Cartesian form.
    2. Draw a vector diagram on graph paper to add (“tip-to-tail”) the thrust and gravitational forces. Draw and label the resultant (net) force vector.

    Vector Diagram

    $$\begin{align}\vec{\mathbf F}_a &= \mathrm{52\ N\ [+65^\circ]} \\ &= \mathrm{(52\cos(+65^\circ), 52\sin(+65^\circ))\ N} \\ &= \mathrm{(+22, +47)\ N} \end{align}$$ $$\begin{align}\vec{\mathbf F}_g &= \mathrm{24\ N\ [-90^\circ]} \\ &= \mathrm{(24\cos(-90^\circ), 24\sin(-90^\circ))\ N} \\ &= \mathrm{(0, -24)\ N} \end{align}$$
