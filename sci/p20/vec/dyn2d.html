Physics 20

Example 1

A student pushes a 2.00 kg box from rest, 2.00 metres up an 8.00° frictionless incline, using an applied force of 10.0 newtons parallel to the ramp. Calculate the work done by both the student and gravity, and determine the speed of the box at the top of the ramp and its acceleration.

$$W_a = \vec{\mathbf F}_a \cdot \Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = \mathrm{10.0\ N\cdot 2.00\ m = 20.0\ J}$$

$$\begin{align}W_g &= |\vec{\mathbf F}_g| \cdot |\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}| \cos(\Delta\theta) \\&= \mathrm{19.6\ N\cdot 2.00\ m\cdot \cos(98.0^\circ)} \\&= \mathrm{-5.46\ J}\end{align}$$

$$\Delta E_k = W_a + W_g = \mathrm{14.5\ J}$$

$$v_f = \sqrt{2E_k\over m} = \mathrm{\sqrt{2(14.5\ J)\over 2.00\ kg} = 3.81\ m/s}$$

$$\vec{\mathbf a} = {v_f^2 - v_i^2\over 2\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{{(3.81\ m/s)^2 \over 2\cdot 2.00\ m} = 3.64\ m/s^2}$$

  • The normal force is not relevant to the solution because it acts perpendicular to the motion and does no work.

Example 2

Calculate the normal force and the acceleration of the box using Newton’s 2nd Law.

  • Use the direction parallel to the ramp as \(x\) and the perpendicular direction as \(y\).
  • Convert the gravity force to Cartesian form: $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{gx} = \mathrm{19.6\ N\cdot \cos(262^\circ) = -2.73\ N}$$ $$\vec{\mathbf F}_{gy} = \mathrm{19.6\ N\cdot \sin(262^\circ) = -19.4\ N}$$
 ∥ [\(x\)]⟂ [\(y\)]
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_a\)10.00.00
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_g\)–2.73–19.4
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_n\)0.00+19.4
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\)+7.270.00
  • The normal force acts only in the perpendicular (\(y\)) direction.
  • We can determine the normal force because the net force acts parallel to the ramp; the \(y\)-component is zero.
  • The normal force balances the perpendicular component of gravity.

$$\vec{\mathbf a}_x = {\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\over m} = \mathrm{{+7.27\ N\over 2.00\ kg} = +3.64\ m/s^2}$$

Example 3

Calculate the speed and acceleration of the box when the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.200.

  • The normal force is relevant now, because it determines how strong the friction force is.

$$F_f = \mu F_n = \mathrm{0.200\cdot 19.4\ N = 3.89\ N}$$

  • The friction force acts only in the negative, parallel (\(x\)) direction.
 ∥ [\(x\)]⟂ [\(y\)]
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_a\)10.00.00
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_g\)–2.73–19.4
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_n\)0.00+19.4
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_f\)–3.890.00
\(\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\)+3.380.00

$$\vec{\mathbf a}_x = {\vec{\mathbf F}_{net}\over m} = \mathrm{{+3.38\ N\over 2.00\ kg} = +1.69\ m/s^2}$$

$$v_f = \sqrt{v_i^2 + 2\vec{\mathbf a}\cdot\Delta\vec{\mathbf d}} = \mathrm{\sqrt{2\cdot 1.69\ m/s^2 \cdot 2.00\ m} = 2.60\ m/s}$$

$$E_k = {1\over 2}mv^2 = \mathrm {{1\over 2} 2.00\ kg (2.60\ m/s)^2 = 6.76\ J}$$

$$W_f = \mathrm{-3.89\ J\cdot 2.00\ m = -7.78\ J}$$
