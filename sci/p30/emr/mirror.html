Derivation

A principal ray diagram for a curved mirror results in several pairs of similar triangles.

  • The ratio of the heights of the similar pairs is equal to the ratio of the bases. $$\frac{-h_i}{h_o} = \color{green}{\frac{d_i}{d_o}} = \color{blue}{\frac{f}{d_o-f}} = \color{red}{\frac{d_i-f}{f}}$$
  • By combining (“cross multiplying”) the blue and green parts of the equation: $$d_o f = d_i d_o - d_i f$$
  • If we now divide this equation by the product of all three variables, \(fd_od_i\): $$\frac{d_o f}{fd_o d_i} = \frac{d_i d_o}{fd_o d_i} - \frac{d_i f}{fd_o d_i}$$ $$\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{d_o}$$
  • The remaining parts of the original equation (black and green) tell us the magnification: $$m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}$$

Example 1

An object is placed 5.0 cm from a concave mirror with a focal length of 3.0 cm. Find the location and properties of the image.

Given:

  • \(f = 3.0\ \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(d_o = 5.0\ \mathrm{cm}\)

Solve for \(d_i\):

$$\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{1}{3.0\ \mathrm{cm}} - \frac{1}{5.0\ \mathrm{cm}} = \frac{2}{15}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-1}$$

Remember that this is the reciprocal of \(d_i\):

$$d_i = \frac{15}{2}\ \mathrm{cm}= 7.5\ \mathrm{cm}$$

Calculate \(m\):

$$m = -\frac{d_i}{d_o} = -\frac{7.5\ \mathrm{cm}}{5.0\ \mathrm{cm}} = -1.5$$

The image is real, inverted, and enlarged.

Draw a ray diagram to confirm the solution.

Example 2

An object is placed 5.0 cm from a convex mirror with a focal length of 3.0 cm. Find the location and properties of the image.

Given:

  • \(f = -3.0\ \mathrm{cm}\)
  • \(d_o = 5.0\ \mathrm{cm}\)

Solve for \(d_i\):

$$\frac{1}{d_i} = \frac{1}{f} - \frac{1}{d_o} = \frac{1}{-3.0\ \mathrm{cm}} - \frac{1}{5.0\ \mathrm{cm}} = \frac{-8}{15}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-1}$$

$$d_i = \frac{15}{-8}\ \mathrm{cm}= -1.9\ \mathrm{cm}$$

Calculate \(m\):

$$m = -\frac{d_i}{d_o} = -\frac{-1.9\ \mathrm{cm}}{5.0\ \mathrm{cm}} = 0.38$$

The image is virtual, upright, and reduced.

Draw a ray diagram to confirm the solution.
