Physics 30

Derivation

The relativistic relation between energy and momentum is: $$E^2 = (mc^2)^2 + (cp)^2$$

For particles with no mass (i.e. photons), this simplifies to:$$E=cp$$

For particles that do have mass, the entire equation must be used when in motion, but at rest (\(p=0\)) it simplifies to:$$E = mc^2$$

For the collision between the photon and the electron at rest, energy conservation states: $$cp_i + mc^2 = cp_s + \left((mc^2)^2 + (cp_e)^2\right)^\frac{1}{2}$$ $$c(p_i - p_s) + mc^2 = \left((mc^2)^2 + (cp_e)^2\right)^\frac{1}{2}$$

Divide both sides by \(c\) and then square both sides: $$(p_i - p_s) + mc = \left((mc)^2 + p_e^2\right)^\frac{1}{2}$$ $$(p_i - p_s)^2 + 2mc(p_i - p_s) + (mc)^2 = (mc)^2 + p_e^2$$

Cancel the \((mc)^2\): $$(p_i - p_s)^2 + 2mc(p_i - p_s) = p_e^2$$

Momentum conservation is described by writing the Law of Cosines for the vector diagram: $$p_e^2 = p_i^2 + p_s^2 - 2p_i p_s\cos\theta$$

Combining equations gives: $$(p_i - p_s)^2 + 2mc(p_i - p_s) = p_i^2 + p_s^2 - 2p_i p_s\cos\theta$$ $$(p_i^2 - 2p_i p_s + p_s^2) + 2mc(p_i - p_s) = p_i^2 + p_s^2 - 2p_i p_s\cos\theta$$

Cancel and collect like terms: $$-2p_i p_s + 2mc(p_i - p_s) = -2p_i p_s\cos\theta$$ $$p_i p_s - mc(p_i - p_s) = p_i p_s\cos\theta$$ $$p_i p_s (1 - \cos\theta) = mc(p_i - p_s)$$

Replace the photon momenta by the wavelength using: $$E = cp = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$ $$p = \frac{h}{\lambda}$$ $$\frac{h^2}{\lambda_i \lambda_s} (1 - \cos\theta) = mc(\frac{h}{\lambda_i} - \frac{h}{\lambda_s})$$

Multiply by \(\lambda_i \lambda_s\): $$h^2 (1 - \cos\theta) = hmc(\lambda_s - \lambda_i)$$ $$h (1 - \cos\theta) = mc(\lambda_s - \lambda_i)$$ $$\frac{h}{mc} (1 - \cos\theta) = \lambda_s - \lambda_i$$
