Science 10 (Physics Lesson )

First Law of Thermodynamics

  • Energy is a conserved quantity.
  • Energy cannot be created or destroyed.
  • Energy can be transformed from one form to another or transferred from one object to another.

Second Law of Thermodynamics

There are several different ways of describing the Second Law.

  • It is not possible to extract all of the thermal energy from a system.
  • The efficiency of any energy transformation will always be less than 100%.
  • Heat flows naturally from higher to lower temperatures.
  • To force heat to flow in the wrong direction (as in a refrigerator or air conditioner) requires adding work to the system.

Efficiency

Efficiency describes how much useful energy is produced by an energy transformation compared to the input energy: $$\epsilon = \left|{\Delta E_{useful} \over \Delta E_{input}}\right|$$

  • The type(s) of energy that are considered “useful” depends on the purpose of the energy transformation.
  • For example, the purpose of a car engine is to move the car, so kinetic energy would be considered “useful” energy.
  • Any sound or thermal energy produced by the engine would be considered “wasted” energy.
  • The input would be the chemical energy of the gasoline, propane, diesel fuel, or battery.
  • Efficiency is often expressed as a percentage; multiply by 100% to convert a decimal to a percentage and divide by 100% for the opposite conversion.

Example

A 50.0 kg skier starts from the top of a 10.0 m tall hill. At the bottom if the hill, her speed is 12.0 m/s. What is the efficiency of the energy transformation?

  • Input is gravitational potential energy:$$E_g = mgh = \mathrm{50.0\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2\cdot 10.0\ m = 4905\ J}$$
  • Useful output is kinetic energy:$$E_k = {1\over 2}mv^2 = \mathrm{{1\over 2}(50.0\ kg) (12.0\ m/s)^2 = 3600\ J}$$
  • Efficiency is:$$\epsilon = \left|{\Delta E_{useful} \over \Delta E_{input}}\right| = \mathrm{3600\ J\over 4905\ J} = 0.734$$

  • 73.4% of the energy input (\(E_g\)) gets transformed into useful energy (\(E_k\)).
  • 26.6% of the energy input (\(E_g\)) gets transformed into waste (sound or thermal energy).
