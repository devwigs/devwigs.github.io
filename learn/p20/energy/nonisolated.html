1.4 – Non-Isolated Systems

Type of Systems

Isolated System: A system that does not exchange either matter or energy with its environment.

Closed System: A system that can exchange energy but not matter with its environment.

Open System: A system that can exchange both energy and matter with its environment.

Energy Conservation

To apply the Law of Energy Conservation, we must account for any energy added or removed from the system.

  • Energy that will be added to the system is included with the initial energy.
  • Energy that has been removed from the system is included with the final energy.

Example

Complete part (a) of the Example on your assignment handout.

A 500-gram ball is dropped from a height of 2.00 metres. When the ball reaches the ground, its speed is measured to be 5.00 m/s.

$$E_{gi} = mgh_i = {\mathrm{0.500\ kg\cdot 9.81\ m/s^2\cdot 2.00\ m = 9.81\ J}}$$

$$E_{kf} = {1\over 2}mv_f^2 = {\mathrm{{1\over 2}0.500\ kg\cdot (5.00\ m/s)^2 = 6.25\ J}}$$

  • The “missing” 3.56 joules is the energy that leaves the system.
  • This energy is the sound and thermal energy produced by the air resistance force.
