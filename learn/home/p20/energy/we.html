• The work done by a force is defined as:$$W = {\vec{\mathbf F}}\cdot \Delta{\vec{\mathbf d}} = |{\vec{\mathbf F}}|\cdot |\Delta{\vec{\mathbf d}}| \cos\Delta\theta$$
  • The work-energy theorem states that the change in an object’s kinetic energy is equal to the total work done by all forces:$$\Delta E_k = \Sigma W$$
  • Work is positive when the angle between the force and the direction of motion is less than 90°, and negative when this angle exceeds 90°.
  • When the force is not constant, work can be calculated as the area under a graph of \(\vec{\mathbf F}\) versus \(\vec{\mathbf d}\).
