The system refers to the objects and energy forms that we are interested in. The environment represents everything outside of the system.

  • An isolated system is one that does not exchange either energy or matter with its environment.
  • A closed system is one that allows energy exchange with its environment but does not exchange matter.
  • An open system permits both energy and matter into or out of the system.

When applying energy conservation to a system that is not isolated, energy entering the system must be added to the initial energy, and energy leaving the system must be added to the final energy.
