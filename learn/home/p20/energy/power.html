Power is the rate at which energy is transformed:$$P = {\Delta E\over \Delta t}$$

  • The SI unit of power is the watt, which is defined as a joule per second.
  • A common but non-SI unit is horsepower; 1 hp = 748 W.

Many energy transformations produce less “useful” energy than they consume; for example an electric light uses more electrical energy than it prooduces in light energy.

  • The energy that is “lost” often appears as thermal or sound energy. These unintentional energy forms are sometimes called “waste”.

Efficiency desribes the useful energy produces as a fraction or percentage of the energy input:$$\epsilon = \left|{\Delta E_{useful}\over \Delta E_{input}}\right|$$
