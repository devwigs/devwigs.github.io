Uniform accelerated motion means motion with constant acceleration.

  • For UAM, the \(\vec{\mathbf v}\)-\(t\) graph will be linear and the \(\vec{\mathbf d}\)-\(t\) graph will be parabola-shaped.
  • For motion that is not UAM, you can “slice” the \(\vec{\mathbf v}\)-\(t\) graph into sections that have constant acceleration and solve each section separately.
  • The area under a \(\vec{\mathbf v}\)-\(t\) graph represents the displacement of the object.

The two basic relations for UAM are: $$\vec{\mathbf a} = {{\vec{\mathbf v}}_f - {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i \over \Delta t}$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = {{\vec{\mathbf v}}_i + {\vec{\mathbf v}}_f \over 2} \Delta t$$

The equations can be combined in different ways to make three new equations: $$v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2 \vec{\mathbf a} \cdot \Delta{\vec{\mathbf d}}$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = {\vec{\mathbf v}}_i \Delta t + {1\over 2}{\vec{\mathbf a}} (\Delta t)^2$$ $$\Delta\vec{\mathbf d} = {\vec{\mathbf v}}_f \Delta t - {1\over 2}{\vec{\mathbf a}} (\Delta t)^2$$

To solve UAM problems:

  • Identify values for three of the five variables.
  • Choose an equation that contains only one of the two unknown variables. It is best to go through the equations in order. Use this equation to find the first unknown.
  • Choose the simplest equation (other than the one already used) to find the second unknown.
