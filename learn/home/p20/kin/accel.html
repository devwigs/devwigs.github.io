Acceleration is the rate at which an object’s velocity changes: $${\vec{\mathbf a}} = {\Delta{\vec{\mathbf v}} \over \Delta t}$$

  • The slope of a \({\vec{\mathbf v}}\) versus \(t\) graph equals the acceleration.
  • When an object moving in 1D is speeding up, its acceleration and velocity will have the same direction.
  • When an object moving in 1D is slowing down, its acceleration and velocity will have opposite directions.
  • Near Earth’s surface, gravity causes an acceleration of 9.81 m/s2 straight down when there are no other forces acting on the object.
