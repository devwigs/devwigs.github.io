The speed of light can be calculated from “time of flight” measurements, such as:

  • Rømer and Huygens’ calculations based on delays in observing eclipses of Jupiter’s moons.
  • Fizeau’s toothed wheel experiments.
  • Michelson’s rotating mirror experiment.

For Michelson’s experiment, the speed of light is determined from the period, \(T\), of the rotating mirror:$$c = {\Delta d \over \Delta t} = {2L \over {T/N}}$$

The best experimental measurements give \(c = 3.00\times 10^8{\mathrm{m/s}}\), in agreement with Maxwell’s prediction for the speed of EMR. This strongly suggests that light is an EM wave.
